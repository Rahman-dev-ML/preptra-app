## Section 1: Algal and Bryophyte Life Cycles

1. Algal and Bryophyte Life Cycles (Hard)
1. In the life cycle of Polysiphonia, if carpospores give rise to tetrasporophytes and tetraspores produce gametophytes, what explains the chromosome number variation in different phases?
A) Meiosis occurs during carpospore formation
B) Meiosis occurs during tetraspore formation
C) Mitosis produces haploid cells
D) Fertilization occurs twice per cycle
Answer: B

2. The evolutionary significance of heteromorphic alternation of generations in brown algae compared to isomorphic patterns suggests:
A) Adaptation to different ecological niches by different phases
B) Reduced genetic variation
C) Simpler reproductive mechanisms
D) Lower metabolic costs
Answer: A

3. In Anthoceros, the unique feature of indeterminate growth of sporophyte with intercalary meristem indicates:
A) Primitive condition linking to vascular plants
B) Advanced condition among bryophytes
C) Convergent evolution with algae
D) Degenerate pteridophyte ancestry
Answer: A

4. The presence of conducting strands in the seta of Polytrichum but not in other mosses suggests:
A) Polyphyletic origin of mosses
B) Parallel evolution of conducting tissues
C) Secondary loss in other mosses
D) Misidentification of cell types
Answer: B

5. If a Chara species shows 89% reduction in chromosome number from zygote to mature plant, this indicates:
A) Regular mitotic divisions
B) Immediate meiosis after fertilization
C) Chromosome elimination during development
D) Polyploid gametophyte formation
Answer: B

6. The evolutionary transition from isogamy to oogamy in Volvocales demonstrates:
A) Increasing cellular differentiation and complexity
B) Decreasing reproductive efficiency
C) Response to terrestrial habitats
D) Reduction in genetic diversity
Answer: A

7. The phenomenon of "false branching" in Scytonema differs from true branching because:
A) It lacks meristematic cells
B) Hormogonia break through sheath laterally
C) Only reproductive cells branch
D) Branches are always unicellular
Answer: B

8. Gemma cups in Marchantia exhibit bilateral symmetry rather than radial, which suggests:
A) Adaptation for directional dispersal
B) Evolutionary link to higher plants
C) Response to gravity
D) Protection from desiccation
Answer: A

9. The reduction of chromosome number in the apophysis cells of moss sporophyte compared to spore mother cells indicates:
A) Somatic chromosome elimination
B) Differential DNA replication
C) Meiosis has not yet occurred
D) Polyploid sporophyte tissue
Answer: C

10. In Ectocarpus, the plurilocular and unilocular sporangia on the same plant producing different spore types demonstrates:
A) Environmental control of reproduction
B) Temporal separation of sexual and asexual cycles
C) Genetic mosaicism
D) Nutritional differences
