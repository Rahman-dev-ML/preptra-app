## Section 1: The Glorious Revolution (1688-1689)

1. The Glorious Revolution (Easy)
1. The Glorious Revolution took place in which year?
A) 1666
B) 1688
C) 1707
D) 1714
Answer: B

A) James II
B) Charles II
C) William III and Mary II
D) George I
Answer: C

3. Which king was overthrown during the Glorious Revolution?
A) Charles I
B) James II
C) Charles II
D) George III
Answer: B

4. The Glorious Revolution is called "glorious" because it was:
A) Very expensive
B) Largely bloodless
C) Led by nobles
D) Supported by France
Answer: B

5. William III was also known as:
A) William of Orange
B) William the Conqueror
C) William the Silent
D) William the Great
Answer: A

6. The Bill of Rights was passed in which year?
A) 1688
B) 1689
C) 1690
D) 1701
Answer: B

7. The Glorious Revolution established the principle of:
A) Absolute monarchy
B) Parliamentary supremacy
C) Divine right of kings
D) Military rule
Answer: B

8. Mary II was the daughter of:
A) Charles II
B) James II
C) William III
D) George I
Answer: B

9. The Glorious Revolution primarily protected which religion?
A) Catholicism
B) Protestantism
C) Judaism
D) Islam
Answer: B

10. The Glorious Revolution marked the end of:
A) Parliament
B) Monarchy
C) Absolute monarchy
D) Democracy
Answer: C
