## Section 1: Invertebrate Zoology - Protozoa and Porifera

1. Protozoa and Porifera (Easy)
1. Protozoa are considered to be:
A) Multicellular
B) Unicellular
C) Tissue-level
D) Organ-level
Answer: B

A) Digestion
B) Osmoregulation
C) Reproduction
D) Movement
Answer: B

A) Asexual reproduction
B) Sexual reproduction
C) Budding
D) Fission
Answer: B

4. Which disease is caused by Trypanosoma?
A) Malaria
B) Sleeping sickness
C) Dysentery
D) Pneumonia
Answer: B

5. Sponges belong to phylum:
A) Cnidaria
B) Porifera
C) Protozoa
D) Platyhelminthes
Answer: B

6. Spicules in sponges are made of:
A) Chitin only
B) Calcium carbonate or silica
C) Protein only
D) Cellulose
Answer: B

7. The simplest canal system in sponges is:
A) Sycon type
B) Leucon type
C) Ascon type
D) Complex type
Answer: C

8. Choanocytes in sponges are also called:
A) Collar cells
B) Nerve cells
C) Muscle cells
D) Gland cells
Answer: A

9. Most sponges are found in:
A) Freshwater only
B) Marine water
C) Terrestrial habitat
D) Air
Answer: B

10. Economic importance of sponges includes:
A) Food source
B) Bath sponges
C) Medicine only
D) Fertilizer
Answer: B
