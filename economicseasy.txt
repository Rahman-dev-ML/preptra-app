## Section 1: Basic Economic Concepts and Definitions

1. Basic Economic Concepts and Definitions (Easy)
1. Economics is the study of:
A) Money only
B) How society manages its scarce resources
C) Stock markets
D) Government policies
Answer: B

2. Microeconomics deals with:
A) National income
B) Individual units and markets
C) Inflation
D) International trade
Answer: B

3. Macroeconomics studies:
A) Individual consumers
B) Single firms
C) The economy as a whole
D) Product pricing
Answer: C

4. Opportunity cost refers to:
A) Money spent
B) The next best alternative forgone
C) Total cost
D) Fixed cost
Answer: B

5. Scarcity means:
A) Poverty
B) Limited resources relative to unlimited wants
C) Shortage of money
D) High prices
Answer: B

6. GDP stands for:
A) Gross Domestic Product
B) General Domestic Price
C) Government Development Plan
D) Global Development Program
Answer: A

7. GNP stands for:
A) Gross National Product
B) General National Price
C) Government National Plan
D) Global National Program
Answer: A

8. The difference between GDP and GNP is:
A) Time period
B) Net factor income from abroad
C) Inflation rate
D) Tax rates
Answer: B

9. Economic goods are:
A) Free goods
B) Scarce goods that have a price
C) Only luxury items
D) Government goods
Answer: B

10. Factors of production include all EXCEPT:
A) Land
B) Labor
C) Money
D) Capital
Answer: C
