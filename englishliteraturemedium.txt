## Section 1: Romantic Poetry Analysis

1. Romantic Poetry Analysis (Medium)
1. How does Wordsworth's treatment of nature differ from 18th-century poets?
A) He sees nature as merely decorative
B) He views nature as a moral teacher and spiritual guide
C) He ignores nature completely
D) He treats nature scientifically
Answer: B

2. Blake's "Songs of Innocence and Experience" demonstrates:
A) Simple children's poetry
B) Dualistic vision of human existence
C) Only optimistic views
D) Pure social criticism
Answer: B

3. Keats' concept of "Negative Capability" means:
A) Inability to write
B) Capacity to remain in uncertainty without irritably reaching after fact
C) Negative criticism
D) Lack of poetic skill
Answer: B

4. Shelley's "Prometheus Unbound" can be read as an allegory of:
A) Greek mythology only
B) Human liberation from tyranny
C) Natural disasters
D) Personal relationships
Answer: B

5. The difference between Wordsworth's early and later poetry shows:
A) No change
B) Movement from radical politics to conservatism
C) Improvement in technique only
D) Decline in quality only
Answer: B

6. Byron's heroes are characterized by:
A) Conventional morality
B) Rebellious individualism and dark passion
C) Religious devotion
D) Social conformity
Answer: B

7. Compare Keats' sensuousness with Shelley's intellectualism:
A) They are identical
B) Keats emphasizes physical beauty while Shelley focuses on abstract ideals
C) Shelley is more sensuous
D) Neither shows these qualities
Answer: B

8. Wordsworth's "spots of time" in The Prelude refer to:
A) Clock watching
B) Moments of spiritual insight that shape development
C) Historical events
D) Daily routines
Answer: B

9. The Lucy poems explore themes of:
A) Urban life
B) Loss, nature, and idealized love
C) Political revolution
D) Scientific progress
Answer: B

10. Coleridge's contribution to Romantic theory includes:
A) Rejection of imagination
B) Distinction between Fancy and Imagination
C) Focus on reason alone
D) Classical rules
Answer: B

11. The Romantic concept of the sublime involves:
A) Beautiful landscapes only
B) Overwhelming experiences that inspire awe and terror
C) Gentle emotions
D) Rational understanding
Answer: B

12. Blake's "London" critiques:
A) Natural beauty
B) Social oppression and institutional corruption
C) Rural life
D) Personal relationships
Answer: B

13. Keats' letters are important because they:
A) Contain gossip
B) Reveal his aesthetic philosophy and poetic development
C) Discuss only personal matters
D) Are poorly written
Answer: B
