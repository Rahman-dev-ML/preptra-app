## Section 1: Advanced Group Theory

1. Group Theory Applications (Medium)
1. If H is a subgroup of G with [G:H] = 2, then H is:
A) Cyclic
B) Abelian
C) Normal in G
D) Simple
Answer: C

2. For the group homomorphism φ: G → G', Im(φ) is isomorphic to:
A) G
B) G'
C) ker(φ)
D) G/ker(φ)
Answer: D

3. If G/Z(G) is cyclic, then G is:
A) Cyclic
B) Abelian
C) Simple
D) Infinite
Answer: B

4. The number of elements of order 2 in Z₂ × Z₂ is:
A) 1
B) 2
C) 3
D) 4
Answer: C

5. Inn(G) ≅ G/Z(G) where Inn(G) denotes:
A) Internal automorphisms
B) Inner automorphisms
C) Inverse automorphisms
D) Identity automorphisms
Answer: B

6. A group of order 15 must be:
A) Non-abelian
B) Simple
C) Cyclic
D) Has a subgroup of order 6
Answer: C

7. If N and M are normal subgroups of G, then NM/M ≅:
A) N/M
B) N/(N∩M)
C) N∪M
D) G/M
Answer: B

8. The alternating group A₄ has order:
A) 6
B) 12
C) 24
D) 48
Answer: B

9. Every group of order p² (p prime) is:
A) Cyclic
B) Simple
C) Abelian
D) Non-abelian
Answer: C

10. If H and K are subgroups with |H| = m, |K| = n, gcd(m,n) = 1, then |H∩K| =:
A) 1
B) mn
C) m+n
D) 0
Answer: A
