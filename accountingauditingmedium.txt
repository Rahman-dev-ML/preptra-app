## Section 1: Financial Statement Preparation

1. Financial Statements (Medium)
1. When preparing financial statements from trial balance, adjusting entries for prepaid expenses will:
A) Increase expenses, decrease assets
B) Decrease expenses, increase assets
C) Increase both expenses and assets
D) Decrease both expenses and assets
Answer: A

2. In preparing a Cash Flow Statement using the indirect method, depreciation is:
A) Subtracted from net income
B) Added back to net income
C) Ignored completely
D) Shown as cash outflow
Answer: B

3. The closing entry for revenue accounts involves:
A) Debit Revenue, Credit Retained Earnings
B) Debit Revenue, Credit Income Summary
C) Credit Revenue, Debit Expense
D) Credit Revenue, Credit Income Summary
Answer: B

4. When bad debt expense is recorded using allowance method:
A) Debit Bad Debt Expense, Credit Accounts Receivable
B) Debit Bad Debt Expense, Credit Allowance for Doubtful Accounts
C) Debit Allowance, Credit Bad Debt Expense
D) Debit Accounts Receivable, Credit Bad Debt
Answer: B

5. Accrued interest revenue at year-end requires:
A) Debit Interest Expense, Credit Cash
B) Debit Cash, Credit Interest Revenue
C) Debit Interest Receivable, Credit Interest Revenue
D) Debit Interest Revenue, Credit Interest Receivable
Answer: C

6. The post-closing trial balance contains only:
A) Revenue and expense accounts
B) Balance sheet accounts
C) Income statement accounts
D) Temporary accounts
Answer: B

7. When inventory is understated, the effect on net income is:
A) Overstated in current year
B) Understated in current year
C) No effect
D) Depends on sales
Answer: B

8. Treasury stock transactions affect:
A) Income statement
B) Equity section only
C) Assets only
D) Liabilities
Answer: B

9. Statement of changes in equity includes all EXCEPT:
A) Net income
B) Dividends
C) Cash flows
D) Share issuances
Answer: C

10. Comprehensive income includes:
A) Only net income
