## Section 1: Theoretical Comparisons and Applications

1. Theoretical Comparisons and Applications (Medium)
1. The main difference between Neo-Realism and Classical Realism lies in their explanation of:
A) State sovereignty
B) The source of conflict (system structure vs human nature)
C) International organizations
D) Economic factors
Answer: B

A) Rejection of cooperation
B) Emphasis on international regimes despite anarchy
C) Focus on military power
D) Dismissal of state actors
Answer: B

3. The convergence between Neo-Realism and Constructivism can be found in their:
A) View on material capabilities
B) Recognition of structure's importance in shaping behavior
C) Emphasis on ideas
D) Rejection of anarchy
Answer: B

4. Modernist IR theories (Realism and Liberalism) differ from post-modernist theories in their:
A) Acceptance of universal truths vs questioning meta-narratives
B) Focus on economics
C) Rejection of state-centrism
D) Emphasis on culture
Answer: A

5. The evolution of IR as an academic discipline was primarily driven by:
A) University expansion
B) Major wars and need to understand international conflict
C) Economic development

Answer: B

6. Constructivism challenges both Realism and Liberalism by arguing that:
A) Power doesn't matter
B) International structure is socially constructed, not given
C) States don't exist
D) Cooperation is impossible
Answer: B

7. The feminist critique of traditional IR theory focuses on:
A) Military strategy only
B) Gender-blind nature of power and security concepts
C) Economic policies
D) Legal frameworks
Answer: B

8. The debate between relative and absolute gains is central to:
A) Marxism vs Capitalism
B) Neo-Realism vs Neo-Liberalism
C) Feminism vs Constructivism
D) Classical vs Modern theory
Answer: B

9. The security dilemma concept illustrates the tension between:
A) Economics and politics
B) Individual state security and systemic instability
C) Democracy and authoritarianism
D) Law and power
Answer: B

A) Balance of power
