## Section 1: Classical Political Thought - Comparative Analysis

1. Classical Political Thought - Comparative Analysis (Medium)
1. The main difference between Hobbes and Locke's concept of State of Nature is:
A) Hobbes sees it as peaceful, Locke as warlike
B) Hobbes sees it as warlike, Locke as relatively peaceful
C) Both see it identically
D) Neither discusses it
Answer: B

2. Plato's Theory of Justice primarily aims to establish:
A) Economic equality
B) Harmony between different classes
C) Democratic governance
D) Individual freedom
Answer: B

3. Aristotle's classification of governments is based on:
A) Economic systems only
B) Number of rulers and their aims
C) Geographic location
D) Military power
Answer: B

4. The concept of General Will in Rousseau differs from majority will because:
A) It represents what is best for the community
B) It is always the majority opinion
C) It is determined by voting
D) It changes frequently
Answer: A

5. Machiavelli's political philosophy is considered revolutionary because:
A) It promoted democracy
B) It separated politics from morality
C) It supported monarchy
D) It advocated socialism
Answer: B

6. Al-Farabi's ideal state combines:
A) Only Islamic principles
B) Greek philosophy with Islamic principles
C) Only Greek philosophy
D) Roman law with Islamic law
Answer: B

7. Ibn Khaldun's concept of Asabiyyah explains:
A) Individual psychology
B) Group solidarity and civilization cycles
C) Economic development
D) Religious movements
Answer: B

8. Marx's historical materialism suggests that:
A) Ideas shape economic conditions
B) Economic conditions shape ideas and politics
C) Religion determines history
D) Great men make history
Answer: B

9. Montesquieu is called the "Aristotle of 18th century" because:
A) He wrote about ethics
B) He classified governments systematically
C) He was Greek
D) He taught Alexander
Answer: B

10. The Social Contract theories differ mainly in their view of:
A) The existence of a contract
B) What rights are surrendered and retained
C) The need for government
D) The definition of justice
Answer: B
