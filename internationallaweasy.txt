## Section 1: Core Concepts and Sources of International Law

1. Core Concepts and Sources (Easy)
1. International Law is primarily concerned with:
A) Individual citizens
B) Relations between states
C) Domestic courts
D) Private companies
Answer: B

2. The father of International Law is considered to be:
A) John Austin
B) Hugo Grotius
C) Hans Kelsen
D) Jeremy Bentham
Answer: B

3. Article 38(1) of the ICJ Statute lists sources of international law. Which is NOT a primary source?
A) Treaties
B) Custom
C) Judicial decisions
D) General principles
Answer: C

4. Pacta Sunt Servanda means:
A) Treaties can be broken
B) Treaties must be kept
C) Treaties are optional
D) Treaties expire
Answer: B

5. Customary international law requires:
A) Written agreement
B) State practice and opinio juris
C) UN approval
D) Judicial decision
Answer: B

6. A treaty is defined as:
A) Any agreement
B) Written agreement between states
C) Oral promise
D) Domestic law
Answer: B

7. Jus cogens refers to:
A) Optional norms
B) Peremptory norms
C) Regional customs
D) Soft law
Answer: B

8. The Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties was adopted in:
A) 1945
B) 1961
C) 1969
D) 1982
Answer: C

9. Opinio juris means:
A) Court opinion
B) Belief that practice is legally required
C) Academic writing
D) State legislation
Answer: B

10. General principles of law refer to:
A) UN resolutions
B) Common principles in domestic legal systems
C) Religious laws
D) Natural law only
Answer: B
