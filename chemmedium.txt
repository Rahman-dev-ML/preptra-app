## Section 1: Acid-Base Concepts, Buffer Action, and Catalysis

1. Acid-Base Concepts, Buffer Action, and Catalysis (Medium)
1. The Lowry-Brønsted concept is less restrictive than Arrhenius because:
A) It only applies to aqueous solutions
B) It includes non-aqueous proton transfer reactions
C) It requires hydroxide ions
D) It only works for strong acids
Answer: B

2. Among H₂SO₄ and HClO₄, the stronger acid is HClO₄ because:
A) It has more oxygen atoms
B) Chlorine is more electronegative than sulfur
C) It has greater resonance stabilization of conjugate base
D) It is a smaller molecule
Answer: C

3. When BCl₃ reacts with diethyl ether, the Lewis acid-base interaction involves:
A) Proton transfer from BCl₃
B) Electron pair donation from oxygen to boron
C) Chloride ion transfer
D) Hydrogen bonding
Answer: B

4. A buffer solution of pH 4.5 can be prepared using acetic acid (pKa = 4.76) by:
A) Using equal concentrations of acid and salt
B) Using more acid than salt
C) Using more salt than acid
D) Using only the acid
Answer: C

5. In acid-base catalysis, the rate enhancement occurs through:
A) Changing the equilibrium constant
B) Lowering the activation energy
C) Increasing the temperature
D) Changing the reaction products
Answer: B

6. Hard acids preferentially bind to hard bases due to:
A) Similar sizes only
B) Electrostatic interactions
C) Covalent bonding
D) Van der Waals forces
Answer: B

7. The pH of a 5.0 × 10⁻² M NaOH solution is:
A) 1.3
B) 12.7
C) 7.0
D) 14.0
Answer: B

8. Symbiosis in HSAB theory refers to:
A) Biological interactions
B) Enhanced stability when similar types combine
C) Decreased stability
D) No interaction
Answer: B

9. H₃PO₄ is a stronger acid than H₃AsO₄ because:
A) P is larger than As
B) P-O bonds are stronger than As-O bonds
C) As is more metallic
D) P has more electrons
Answer: B

10. The Henderson-Hasselbalch equation is most accurate when:
A) pH << pKa
B) pH >> pKa
C) pH ≈ pKa
