## Section 1: Anthropological Theory and Methods

1. Anthropological Theory and Methods (Medium)
1. The main difference between qualitative and quantitative research methods in anthropology is:
A) Qualitative is easier than quantitative
B) Qualitative seeks meaning and context while quantitative seeks statistical patterns
C) Only quantitative is scientific
D) They are essentially the same
Answer: B

2. Objectivity versus subjectivity in anthropological research reflects the tension between:
A) Good and bad research
B) Scientific detachment and cultural understanding through empathy
C) Male and female researchers
D) Modern and traditional methods
Answer: B

3. The distinction between technique, method, and methodology in anthropology is:
A) There is no difference
B) Technique is a tool, method is procedure, methodology is overall approach
C) They are interchangeable terms
D) Only methodology matters
Answer: B

4. Thick description, as proposed by Clifford Geertz, means:
A) Writing long ethnographies
B) Detailed analysis of cultural meanings behind observed behaviors
C) Statistical analysis
D) Surface-level observation
Answer: B

5. The emic-etic distinction is important because:
A) It shows insider versus outsider perspectives on culture
B) It is outdated
C) Only emic matters
D) Only etic is scientific
Answer: A

6. Participant observation paradoxically requires:
A) Complete detachment
B) Both involvement and analytical distance
C) No participation
D) Only observation
Answer: B

7. Ethnographic present tense has been criticized because:
A) It's grammatically incorrect
B) It presents cultures as unchanging and timeless
C) It's too modern
D) It's confusing
Answer: B

8. Reflexivity in anthropological research means:
A) Using mirrors
B) Acknowledging how the researcher affects the research
C) Automatic responses
D) Quick thinking
Answer: B

9. Multi-sited ethnography emerged in response to:
A) Lack of funding
B) Globalization and transnational connections
C) Researcher preferences
D) Government requirements
Answer: B

10. The crisis of representation in anthropology refers to:
A) Artistic problems
B) Questions about authority to speak for others
C) Photography issues
D) Map-making difficulties
Answer: B
