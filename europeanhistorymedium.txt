## Section 1: French Revolution - Causes and Consequences

1. French Revolution - Causes and Consequences (Medium)
1. The French Revolution was precipitated by economic factors primarily because:
A) The nobility refused to pay taxes
B) The burden of debt from wars and financial mismanagement created a fiscal crisis
C) The bourgeoisie wanted lower taxes
D) Trade with Britain was blocked
Answer: B

2. The Declaration of the Rights of Man differed from the American Declaration of Independence by:
A) Emphasizing universal natural rights rather than specific grievances
B) Focusing only on French citizens
C) Excluding property rights
D) Supporting monarchy
Answer: A

3. The Reign of Terror represented a paradox because:
A) It promoted peace through war
B) It violated revolutionary ideals of liberty while claiming to defend them
C) It supported monarchy while opposing it
D) It united all social classes
Answer: B

4. The Directory (1795-1799) failed primarily due to:
A) Foreign invasion
B) Lack of popular legitimacy and political instability
C) Economic prosperity
D) Too much democracy
Answer: B

5. The Civil Constitution of the Clergy (1790) was significant because it:
A) Strengthened the Catholic Church
B) Created a fundamental split between revolution and traditional religion
C) United all French citizens
D) Was accepted by the Pope
Answer: B

6. The Tennis Court Oath demonstrated:
A) Royal authority
B) The Third Estate's determination to create constitutional government
C) Military power
D) Religious unity
Answer: B

7. The sans-culottes differed from the Jacobins in:
A) Supporting the monarchy
B) Representing working-class radicalism versus middle-class leadership
C) Opposing all reforms
D) Being more conservative
Answer: B

8. The revolutionary calendar's significance lay in:
A) Better timekeeping
B) Attempting to break with Christian tradition and create a new rational order
C) Honoring the king
D) Improving agriculture
Answer: B

9. The Law of Maximum (1793) demonstrated:
A) Free market principles
B) Revolutionary government's intervention in economy during crisis
C) Support for merchants
D) International trade promotion
Answer: B

10. Women's role in the French Revolution revealed:
A) Complete equality achievement
B) Tension between revolutionary ideals and gender limitations
C) No participation
D) Leadership positions
Answer: B
