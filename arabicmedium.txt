## Section 1: Classical Arabic Poetry Analysis

1. Classical Arabic Poetry Analysis (Medium)
1. In Ka'b ibn Zuhayr's "Banat Su'ad," the mention of Su'ad serves as:
A) Only a love interest
B) A symbolic prelude to the main theme
C) The central theme throughout
D) A historical reference
Answer: B

2. The transformation of the qasida during the Abbasid period was primarily due to:
A) Persian influence alone
B) Urban civilization and cultural diversity
C) Loss of Arabic identity
D) Government mandate
Answer: B

3. Al-Mutanabbi's epithet suggests he:
A) Was a prophet
B) Made prophetic claims in his poetry
C) Wrote religious poetry only
D) Opposed prophecy
Answer: B

4. The nasib in pre-Islamic qasidas typically transitions to the main theme through:
A) Abrupt change
B) The journey motif (rahil)
C) Religious invocation
D) Direct address
Answer: B

5. When analyzing the line "لسان الفتى نصف ونصف فؤاده", the poet emphasizes:
A) Physical appearance
B) Balance between speech and heart
C) Language skills only
D) Emotional state only
Answer: B

6. The verses "ولد الهدى فالكائنات ضياء" celebrate:
A) A military victory
B) The Prophet's birth
C) A caliph's coronation
D) A poet's achievement
Answer: B

7. In pre-Islamic poetry, the phrase "أيام العرب" in poetic context refers to:
A) Daily life descriptions
B) Battle day narratives
C) Religious celebrations
D) Market gatherings
Answer: B

8. The structural difference between Jahili and Islamic poetry lies mainly in:
A) Meter and rhyme
B) Thematic content and moral purpose
C) Length only
D) Language complexity
Answer: B

9. Hassan ibn Thabit's poetry during the Islamic period focused on:
A) Maintaining pre-Islamic themes
B) Defending Islam and the Prophet
C) Nature poetry exclusively
D) Love poetry only
Answer: B

10. The Mu'allaqat's significance lies in their:
A) Religious content
B) Representation of peak pre-Islamic artistry
C) Simple language
D) Short length
Answer: B
