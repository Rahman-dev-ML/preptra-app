## Section 1: Nature and Scope of Criminology

1. Nature and Scope of Criminology (Easy)
1. Criminology is the scientific study of:
A) Law only
B) Crime and criminal behavior
C) Psychology only
D) Sociology only
Answer: B

2. The scope of criminology includes:
A) Criminal law only
B) Forensics only
C) Criminal law, forensics, and criminal investigation
D) Psychology only
Answer: C

3. Criminology as a scientific discipline developed during:
A) Ancient times
B) Medieval period
C) 18th-19th centuries
D) 21st century
Answer: C

4. The importance of criminology in Pakistan has been:
A) Decreasing
B) Increasing
C) Remaining constant
D) Non-existent
Answer: B

5. Crime is different from sin because:
A) They are the same
B) Crime violates legal codes, sin violates religious codes
C) Sin is more serious
D) Crime is not punishable
Answer: B

6. Deviance refers to:
A) Criminal behavior only
B) Behavior that violates social norms
C) Legal behavior
D) Religious behavior only
Answer: B

7. A vice is best described as:
A) A serious crime
B) A moral failing or bad habit
C) A legal requirement
D) A social norm
Answer: B

8. Crime is considered a major social problem because:
A) It affects society's well-being
B) It is rare
C) It only affects individuals
D) It is natural
Answer: A

9. The study of criminal etiology focuses on:
A) Punishment
B) Causes of crime
C) Court procedures
D) Police work
Answer: B

10. Penology is the study of:
A) Crime causation
B) Criminal investigation
C) Punishment and corrections
D) Criminal law
Answer: C

18. Phrenology is the study of:
A) Fingerprints
