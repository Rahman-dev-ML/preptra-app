## Section 1: Invertebrate Zoology - Advanced Concepts

1. Invertebrate Complexity (Medium)
1. The transition from ascon to leucon canal system in sponges represents:
A) Degeneration
B) Increased surface area for water filtration
C) Simpler structure
D) Loss of function
Answer: B

2. Polymorphism in Obelia serves to:
A) Confuse predators
B) Division of labor between polyp and medusa
C) Increase size only
D) No specific function
Answer: B

3. The success of Arthropoda is attributed to all EXCEPT:
A) Jointed appendages
B) Segmented body
C) Soft exoskeleton
D) Diverse habitats
Answer: C

4. Torsion in gastropods results in:
A) Symmetrical body
B) Anterior positioning of mantle cavity
C) Loss of shell
D) Increased speed
Answer: B

5. Water vascular system in echinoderms functions for:
A) Locomotion only
B) Locomotion, respiration, and food capture
C) Excretion only
D) Reproduction only
Answer: B

6. Flame cells and nephridia differ in:
A) Function only
B) Structure and evolutionary advancement
C) Location only
D) No difference
Answer: B

7. The coelom in annelids versus arthropods:
A) Same type and function
B) True coelom vs reduced haemocoel
C) No coelom in either
D) Pseudocoelom in both
Answer: B

8. Metameric segmentation provides advantage of:
A) Increased body size only
B) Locomotor efficiency and redundancy
C) Decreased flexibility
D) No advantage
Answer: B

9. Parasitic platyhelminthes show tegument instead of epidermis for:
A) Beauty
B) Protection from host enzymes
C) Increased absorption
D) Both B and C
Answer: D

10. The evolutionary significance of cephalization is:
A) Directional movement and sensory concentration
B) Increased body size
C) Better reproduction
D) Improved digestion
Answer: A
