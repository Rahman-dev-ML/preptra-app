## Section 1: Plate Tectonics and Earth Structure

1. Plate Tectonics and Earth Structure (Medium)
1. The D" (Dee double prime) layer is significant because it:
A) Is the source of all earthquakes
B) Represents the boundary between the mantle and outer core
C) Contains all Earth's water
D) Is where plates originate
Answer: B

2. Plate tectonics is considered a unifying theory because it explains:
A) Only earthquakes
B) Multiple geological phenomena including earthquakes, volcanism, and mountain building
C) Ocean currents only
D) Climate change
Answer: B

3. Transform fault boundaries are characterized by:
A) Plates moving apart
B) Plates sliding past each other horizontally
C) Plates colliding head-on
D) No plate movement
Answer: B

4. The relationship between earthquake depth and plate boundaries shows:
A) All earthquakes occur at the same depth
B) Deeper earthquakes typically occur at subduction zones
C) Surface earthquakes only occur at transform boundaries
D) Depth has no relation to boundary type
Answer: B

5. The Pacific Ring of Fire exists because:
A) Of ocean currents
B) Of extensive subduction zones around the Pacific Plate
C) The Pacific is the largest ocean
D) Of volcanic hot spots only
Answer: B

6. The process of seafloor spreading provides evidence for plate tectonics through:
A) Ocean depth only
B) Magnetic striping patterns parallel to mid-ocean ridges
C) Fish migration patterns
D) Ocean temperature
Answer: B

7. The Wilson Cycle describes:
A) Ocean currents
B) The opening and closing of ocean basins over geological time
C) Daily tides
D) Seasonal changes
Answer: B

8. Isostasy explains why:
A) Oceans are blue
B) Mountains have roots and continents float on the mantle
C) Earthquakes occur
D) Volcanoes erupt
Answer: B

9. The asthenosphere's properties allow for:
A) Rigid plate behavior
B) Plate movement due to its plastic behavior
C) No movement
D) Only vertical movement
Answer: B

10. Hot spots differ from plate boundaries because they:
A) Are cooler
B) Remain stationary while plates move over them
C) Only occur on land
D) Don't produce volcanoes
Answer: B
