## Section 1: Evolution and Decline of Muslim Society

1. Evolution and Decline of Muslim Society (Medium)
1. The primary factor that distinguished Akbar's religious policy from Aurangzeb's was:
A) Military expansion
B) Synthesis versus orthodoxy approach
C) Economic policies
D) Foreign relations
Answer: B

2. The decline of Muslim society in the 18th century can be attributed to multiple factors. Which combination best explains this decline?
A) External invasions only
B) Weak successors, administrative decay, and regional assertions
C) Religious persecution only
D) Economic factors alone
Answer: B

3. Shah Waliullah's movement differed from earlier reform movements in that it:
A) Rejected all change
B) Attempted to reconcile Islamic law with changing times
C) Promoted Western education
D) Focused only on military reform
Answer: B

4. The impact of the Marathas and Sikhs on Mughal decline was significant because:
A) They were better administrators
B) They exploited internal weaknesses and created regional power centers
C) They had foreign support
D) They introduced new technology
Answer: B

5. The transformation from Delhi Sultanate to Mughal Empire represented:
A) Mere change of rulers
B) Shift from Turkish-Afghan to Central Asian political culture
C) Religious conversion
D) Economic revolution
Answer: B

6. The Mughal administrative system's strength lay in:
A) Military power only
B) Synthesis of Persian administration with local traditions
C) Religious orthodoxy
D) Isolation from locals
Answer: B

7. The role of Sufi silsilahs in Muslim society was crucial because they:
A) Opposed all rulers
B) Bridged the gap between rulers and masses through spiritual influence
C) Focused on politics only
D) Remained isolated
Answer: B

8. The economic decline of Muslim society in the 18th century was accelerated by:
A) Natural disasters only
B) Drain of wealth through constant wars and luxury consumption
C) Trade monopoly
D) Agricultural surplus
Answer: B

9. The cultural synthesis during the Mughal period resulted in:
A) Pure Islamic culture
B) Indo-Islamic composite culture
C) Hindu dominance
D) Western influence
Answer: B

10. The failure of later Mughals compared to early emperors was primarily due to:
A) Lack of military power
B) Inability to maintain balance between diverse groups
C) Foreign invasions
D) Religious differences
Answer: B
