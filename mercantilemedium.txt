## Section 1: Contract Law Applications

1. Contract Law Applications (Medium)
1. When Jamal threatens to commit suicide unless his wife signs a property transfer, this constitutes:
A) Valid consent
B) Coercion making the contract voidable
C) Undue influence only
D) No legal effect
Answer: B

2. A typing error in a tender notification awarding a contract to the wrong party results in:
A) Binding contract with wrong party
B) No contract due to lack of consensus ad idem
C) Contract with both parties
D) Criminal liability
Answer: B

3. If a seller knows about a latent defect in goods but remains silent, this constitutes:
A) Innocent non-disclosure
B) Fraudulent concealment
C) Valid sales practice
D) Breach of warranty only
Answer: B

4. When performance becomes impossible due to change in law, the contract is:
A) Breached
B) Frustrated and discharged
C) Valid and enforceable
D) Voidable only
Answer: B

5. A contract obtained by threatening criminal prosecution is voidable due to:
A) Mistake
B) Coercion
C) Fraud
D) Misrepresentation
Answer: B

6. When parties agree to substitute a new contract for an existing one, this is:
A) Alteration
B) Novation
C) Rescission only
D) Breach
Answer: B

7. If a minor fraudulently represents himself as major and enters a contract:
A) Contract is valid
B) Minor remains protected despite fraud
C) Minor liable in tort
D) Contract becomes criminal
Answer: B

8. Quantum meruit applies when:
A) Full contract is performed
B) Work done without agreed price or under void contract
C) Contract is breached
D) Payment is made in advance
Answer: B

9. A agrees to sell his car to B for Rs. 500,000. Unknown to both, the car was destroyed in an accident. The contract is:
A) Valid
B) Void due to impossibility
C) Voidable
D) Illegal
Answer: B

10. When time is specified as essence of contract and not performed within time:
A) Automatic extension granted
B) Contract becomes voidable at option of promisee
C) Contract remains valid
D) Criminal liability arises
Answer: B
