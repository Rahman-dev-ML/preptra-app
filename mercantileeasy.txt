## Section 1: Contract Law Fundamentals

1. Contract Law Fundamentals (Easy)
1. A contract is defined as:
A) A promise
B) An agreement enforceable by law
C) A social arrangement
D) A written document
Answer: B

2. For a valid contract, free consent means consent not obtained by:
A) Proper explanation
B) Coercion, undue influence, fraud, misrepresentation, or mistake
C) Written agreement
D) Witnesses
Answer: B

3. When consent is obtained by coercion, the contract is:
A) Void
B) Voidable
C) Valid
D) Illegal
Answer: B

4. A void agreement is:
A) Enforceable by law
B) Not enforceable by law
C) Partially enforceable
D) Criminal
Answer: B

5. Consideration in a contract means:
A) Thinking about the contract
B) Something in return
C) Kindness
D) Respect
Answer: B

6. The general rule "No consideration, no contract" has exceptions including:
A) Business agreements
B) Natural love and affection between near relatives
C) Commercial transactions
D) Verbal promises
Answer: B

7. A mistake of fact in a contract makes it:
A) Valid
B) Void
C) Criminal
D) Permanent
Answer: B

8. Misrepresentation differs from fraud in that misrepresentation:
A) Is always intentional
B) Lacks intent to deceive
C) Is more serious
D) Cannot be remedied
Answer: B

9. Breach of contract occurs when:
A) A party performs the contract
B) A party fails to perform obligations
C) Contract is signed
D) Contract expires naturally
Answer: B

10. Quantum meruit means:
A) Large amount
B) As much as earned
C) Fixed price
D) No payment
Answer: B
