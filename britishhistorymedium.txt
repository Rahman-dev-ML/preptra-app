## Section 1: Constitutional Development and Revolution

1. Constitutional Development and Revolution (Medium)
1. The Glorious Revolution's most significant constitutional outcome was:
A) Abolition of Parliament
B) Establishment of parliamentary sovereignty over the Crown
C) Creation of a republic
D) Divine right of kings reinforcement
Answer: B

2. The Bill of Rights (1689) differed from Magna Carta (1215) primarily in:
A) Both limited royal power equally
B) Bill of Rights established parliamentary supremacy while Magna Carta protected baronial privileges
C) Magna Carta was more comprehensive
D) Neither had lasting impact
Answer: B

3. The Hanoverian Succession (1714) was necessary because:
A) Queen Anne died without Protestant heirs
B) Parliament wanted German rulers
C) Scotland demanded it
D) France threatened invasion
Answer: A

4. The Act of Settlement (1701) primarily aimed to:
A) Settle colonial disputes
B) Ensure Protestant succession to the throne
C) Resolve parliamentary seats
D) End the civil war
Answer: B

5. The significance of the Glorious Revolution for American colonists was:
A) It had no impact on colonies
B) It established precedent for resistance to arbitrary rule
C) It increased colonial taxation
D) It ended colonial self-government
Answer: B

6. The Whig interpretation of history viewed the Glorious Revolution as:
A) A disaster for progress
B) The inevitable triumph of liberty and parliamentary democracy
C) Unimportant event
D) Foreign invasion
Answer: B

7. The financial settlement after 1689 gave Parliament control through:
A) Complete abolition of royal income
B) Annual approval of military budgets
C) Eliminating all taxes
D) Creating the Bank of England immediately
Answer: B

8. The Triennial Act (1694) was significant because it:
A) Required Parliament to meet every three years
B) Limited monarchs to three-year reigns
C) Created three houses of Parliament
D) Established three-party system
Answer: A

9. James II's downfall was primarily due to:
A) Military weakness alone
B) Combination of Catholic policies and birth of Catholic heir
C) Economic failure
D) Scottish rebellion
Answer: B

10. The Convention Parliament of 1689 was unique because:
A) It included women
B) It was summoned without royal writ
C) It met in Scotland
D) It had three houses
Answer: B
