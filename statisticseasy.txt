## Section 1: Probability Theory

1. Probability Theory (Easy)
1. The probability of an event always lies between:
A) -1 and 1
B) 0 and 1

Answer: B

B) 0.5

Answer: C

3. The complement of an event A is denoted by:
A) A'
B) A²

A) 0.6
B) 0.4

D) -0.6
Answer: B

5. The classical definition of probability assumes:

B) Equally likely outcomes

Answer: B

A) 1/6
B) 1/3

B) P(A∩B) = P(A) × P(B)
C) P(A∩B) = P(A) + P(B)

9. The sum of probabilities of all possible outcomes equals:
A) 0
B) 0.5
C) 1
