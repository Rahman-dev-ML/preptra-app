## Section 1: Research Methods and Ethics

1. Research Methods and Ethics (Medium)
1. The difference between a hypothesis and a theory is that:
A) They are identical concepts
B) A hypothesis is a testable prediction while a theory is a broad explanation supported by evidence
C) Theories come before hypotheses
D) Hypotheses cannot be tested
Answer: B

2. In an experiment studying the effect of caffeine on memory, caffeine is the:
A) Dependent variable
B) Independent variable
C) Confounding variable
D) Control variable
Answer: B

3. A double-blind study is one where:
A) Participants wear blindfolds
B) Neither participants nor researchers know who receives treatment
C) Only participants are unaware
D) The study is conducted twice
Answer: B

4. External validity refers to:
A) Whether results can be generalized to other populations
B) Internal consistency
C) Statistical significance
D) Ethical approval
Answer: A

5. The Hawthorne effect demonstrates that:
A) Light improves productivity
B) People may change behavior when they know they're being observed
C) Rewards always increase performance
D) Groups work better than individuals
Answer: B

6. Informed consent requires all EXCEPT:
A) Voluntary participation
B) Understanding of procedures
C) Deception about the study's purpose
D) Right to withdraw
Answer: C

7. The Stanford Prison Experiment raised ethical concerns about:
A) Deception only
B) Psychological harm and role-playing effects
C) Informed consent only
D) Payment of participants
Answer: B

8. A correlation coefficient of -0.85 indicates:
A) No relationship
B) Strong positive relationship
C) Strong negative relationship
D) Measurement error
Answer: C

9. Random assignment helps to:
A) Increase external validity
B) Control for participant differences
C) Ensure ethical treatment
D) Reduce sample size needed
Answer: B

10. The replication crisis in psychology refers to:
A) Too many studies being conducted
B) Difficulty in reproducing research findings
C) Lack of funding
D) Ethical violations
Answer: B
