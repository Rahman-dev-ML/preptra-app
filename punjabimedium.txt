## Section 1: Folk Literature Analysis

1. Folk Literature Analysis (Medium)
1. The folk song "Dhola" is considered ancient because it:
A) Has simple language
B) Reflects pre-Islamic cultural elements
C) Is short in length
D) Uses modern themes
Answer: B

2. Punjabi folk songs preserve social history by:
A) Direct historical narration
B) Embedding cultural values and practices
C) Government documentation
D) Written chronicles
Answer: B

3. The difference between "Mahiya" and "Dhola" lies in:
A) Language only
B) Length and thematic scope
C) Religious content
D) Modern vs ancient
Answer: B

4. Wedding songs (Geet) in Punjabi culture function as:
A) Entertainment only
B) Social commentary and ritual markers
C) Religious obligations
D) Economic transactions
Answer: B

5. The performance tradition of "Boli" involves:
A) Solo recitation
B) Competitive improvisation
C) Written scripts
D) Silent meditation
Answer: B

6. Folk songs reflect the "taqaddus" (sanctity) of relationships through:
A) Direct preaching
B) Metaphorical representations
C) Legal documents
D) Foreign influences
Answer: B

7. The evolution of "Jugni" shows:
A) Static tradition
B) Adaptive narrative framework
C) Decline in quality
D) Foreign origin
Answer: B

8. "Lori" as the world's most ancient folk song suggests:
A) Punjabi superiority
B) Universal human experience of motherhood
C) Historical accident
D) Modern invention
Answer: B

9. The social function of "Alahnia" (mourning songs) is to:
A) Increase sadness
B) Provide cathartic expression
C) Prevent mourning
D) Entertainment purpose
Answer: B

10. Regional variations in folk songs indicate:
A) Corruption of tradition
B) Cultural diversity within Punjab
C) External influence only
D) Modern changes
Answer: B
