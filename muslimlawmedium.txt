## Section 1: Schools of Jurisprudence

1. Schools of Jurisprudence (Medium)
1. The main difference between Imam Shafi'i and Hanafi methodology lies in their approach to:
A) Quran interpretation
B) Hadith authentication and use of Istihsan
C) Prayer methods
D) Inheritance rules
Answer: B

2. The concept of Talfiq (combining opinions from different schools) is:
A) Universally accepted
B) Controversial among traditional scholars
C) Mandatory in modern times
D) Forbidden in Quran
Answer: B

3. Imam Abu Hanifa's methodology was characterized by extensive use of:
A) Weak hadith
B) Ra'y (opinion) and Qiyas
C) Only Quran
D) Dreams and visions
Answer: B

4. The Zahiri school, founded by Dawud al-Zahiri, is known for:
A) Extensive use of analogy
B) Literal interpretation without analogy
C) Mystical interpretation
D) Rejection of Hadith
Answer: B

5. The term "Ahl al-Hadith" is most closely associated with which school?
A) Hanafi
B) Maliki
C) Shafi'i and Hanbali
D) All schools equally
Answer: C

6. Imam Malik's Al-Muwatta is significant because it:
A) Rejects all hadith
B) Combines hadith with Medinan practice
C) Only contains Quranic verses
D) Focuses on philosophy
Answer: B

7. The concept of "Amal Ahl al-Madina" (Practice of Medinan people) is specific to:
A) Hanafi school
B) Maliki school
C) Shafi'i school
D) Hanbali school
Answer: B

8. Neo-Ijtihad movements in the modern era advocate for:
A) Strict adherence to one school
B) Direct interpretation beyond traditional schools
C) Abandoning Islamic law
D) Creating a fifth school
Answer: B

9. The Hanafi school's acceptance in the Ottoman Empire was due to:
A) Its flexibility in administrative matters
B) Military conquest
C) Rejection of other schools
D) Simplicity only
Answer: A

10. The phenomenon of "Madhhab fanaticism" (Ta'assub Madhhabi) led to:
A) Unity among Muslims
B) Scholarly debates and sometimes conflicts
C) Abandonment of schools
D) Creation of new religions
Answer: B
