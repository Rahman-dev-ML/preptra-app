## Section 1: Communication Theories and Models

1. Communication Theories and Models (Easy)
1. The SMCR model stands for:
A) Source, Message, Channel, Receiver
B) Sender, Medium, Content, Response
C) Signal, Media, Communication, Reception
D) System, Message, Code, Result
Answer: A

2. Who developed the Mathematical/Shannon-Weaver Model of communication?
A) Harold Lasswell
B) Claude Shannon and Warren Weaver
C) Wilbur Schramm
D) Marshall McLuhan
Answer: B

3. The concept of "Global Village" was introduced by:
A) Wilbur Schramm
B) Harold Lasswell
C) Marshall McLuhan
D) Paul Lazarsfeld
Answer: C

4. In the Two-Step Flow Theory, information flows from media to:
A) Directly to all audiences
B) Opinion leaders, then to audiences
C) Government, then to public
D) Advertisers, then to consumers
Answer: B

5. The Hypodermic Needle Theory suggests that media messages:
A) Are filtered by audiences
B) Have direct and immediate effect
C) Are always rejected
D) Need multiple exposures
Answer: B

6. Feedback in communication refers to:
A) The original message
B) The response from receiver
C) The channel used
D) The source of information
Answer: B

7. Noise in communication process means:
A) Loud sounds only
B) Any interference in message transmission
C) Background music
D) Voice quality
Answer: B

8. The basic elements of communication are:
A) Three
B) Four
C) Five
D) Six
Answer: C

9. Interpersonal communication involves:
A) Mass audiences
B) Two or more people
C) Only written messages
D) Broadcasting only
Answer: B

10. Mass communication differs from interpersonal communication in:
A) Message clarity
B) Audience size and feedback
C) Language used
D) Time taken
Answer: B
