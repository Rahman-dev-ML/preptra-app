# 2001-2005

### **Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in Geography Papers (2001--2005)**

**Note:** Questions are categorized by recurring themes, with years
specified.

### **Paper-I: Physical Geography**

#### **1. Ocean Currents**

1.  **Pattern:** Focus on types, causes, and major currents
    (Atlantic/Pacific).

2.  2001 Q1: \"Highlight the currents of the Atlantic Ocean.\"

3.  2002 Q5: \"Comparative study of Gulf Stream and Kuroshio Current.\"

4.  2005 Q6: \"Discuss the currents of the Pacific Ocean.\"

#### **2. Climate Types**

1.  **Pattern:** Mediterranean and Monsoon climates are recurring.

2.  2003 Q1: \"Discuss Mediterranean climate (characteristics and
    regional variations).\"

3.  2004 Q3: \"Monsoon Climatic Region in detail.\"

#### **3. Geological Processes**

1.  **Pattern:** Glaciers, earthquakes, and weathering.

2.  2001 Q3: \"Define glacier\... features of a glaciated region.\"

3.  2003 Q2: \"Landforms associated with glaciation.\"

4.  2001 Q2 & 2003 Q4: Earthquakes (causes, zones, effects).

#### **4. Atmospheric Phenomena**

1.  **Pattern:** Cyclones, tides, and winds.

2.  2001 Q5: \"Characteristics of thunderstorms.\"

3.  2002 Q1: \"Origin and life history of extra-tropical cyclones.\"

4.  2003 Q3: \"Types of tides.\"

5.  2005 Q2: \"Formation of mountain/valley winds.\"

#### **5. Rocks and Landforms**

1.  **Pattern:** Classification of rocks (igneous, sedimentary,
    metamorphic) and landform evolution.

2.  2004 Q1: \"Igneous rocks: intrusive vs. extrusive.\"

3.  2005 Q3: \"Classify rocks and discuss formation.\"

#### **6. Map Projections and Techniques**

1.  **Pattern:** Types, uses, and comparisons of projections.

2.  2001 Q7(b): \"Boone's modified Conical Projection.\"

3.  2002 Q6: \"Aerial photographs vs. topographical maps.\"

4.  2005 Q7: \"Map projections under major types (characteristics and
    uses).\"

### **Paper-II: Human and Regional Geography**

#### **1. Urbanization and Population**

1.  **Pattern:** Global trends, demographic transition, and regional
    disparities.

2.  2001 Q1: \"History and present pattern of world urbanization.\"

3.  2002 Q2: \"Demographic transition in USA/Canada.\"

4.  2004 Q5: \"Types of migration.\"

#### **2. Economic Activities**

1.  **Pattern:** Agriculture, industry, and resource distribution.

2.  2001 Q2: \"World pattern of economic activities (Pakistan focus).\"

3.  2003 Q4: \"Hydroelectricity development factors.\"

4.  2005 Q4: \"Agriculture as linchpin of South Asia's economy
    (Pakistan).\"

#### **3. Regional Studies**

1.  **Pattern:** South Asia, Middle East, and Europe.

2.  2001 Q5: \"Power resources of South Asia.\"

3.  2002 Q5: \"Ancient river-based regional structure of South Asia.\"

4.  2005 Q2: \"Petroleum production in the Middle East.\"

#### **4. Pakistan-Specific Topics**

1.  **Pattern:** Strategic, economic, and physiographic focus.

2.  2003 Q5: \"Physiographic features of Pakistan.\"

3.  2003 Q7(b): \"Strategic importance of Pakistan.\"

4.  2005 Q7(a): \"Gwadar Port.\"

#### **5. Environmental Issues**

1.  **Pattern:** Determinism vs. possibilism, environmental stress.

2.  2002 Q1: \"Environmental stress in regional studies.\"

3.  2004 Q1: \"Environmental Determinism vs. Possibilism.\"

### **Compulsory Questions (MCQs)**

#### **Repeating Themes:**

1.  **Terminology:***Sedimentary rocks* (2001, 2003, 2005).

2.  *Map projections* (Conical, Zenithal, Mercator) (2001--2005).

3.  **Geographical Facts:**Earth's water coverage (\~71%) (2001, 2003).

4.  Capitals (e.g., Tirana, La Paz) (2001--2005).

5.  Specific features (e.g., stalactites, stalagmites) (2001, 2003).

6.  **Pakistan-Specific Data:**Indus River length (2004, 2005).

7.  Urban population percentage (2004, 2005).

### **Key Takeaways**

1.  **High-Frequency Topics:**Ocean currents, Mediterranean/Monsoon
    climates, glaciers, cyclones, map projections.

2.  Urbanization, economic activities, Pakistan's geography.

3.  **Critical Years for Overlaps:**2003: Focus on glaciation and
    Pakistan's physiography.

4.  2005: Emphasis on Middle East oil and South Asian agriculture.

5.  **Preparation Tips:**Master definitions (e.g., igneous rocks,
    cyclones).

6.  Memorize key data points (e.g., Earth's water coverage, Pakistan's
    population stats).

7.  Practice map-based questions (projections, currents).

6.  **Regional Agriculture/Industry**:

7.  MCQs on cotton, jute, rice bowl regions (2009, 2010).

8.  Essays: \"Cotton production in South Asia\" (2011), \"Power
    resources in Pakistan\" (2009).

#### **4. Recurring MCQ Topics**

1.  **Founders of Geography**: Eratosthenes, Huntington, Christaller
    (2009, 2011).

2.  **Climate Terms**: Albedo, doldrums, Coriolis force (2009, 2010).

3.  **Rock Types**: Igneous (granite), sedimentary (sandstone),
    metamorphic (2008, 2010).

4.  **Pakistan-Specific Facts**:

5.  Hottest city (Jacobabad), longest river (Indus), borders (2009,
    2011).

#### **5. Essay Patterns**

1.  **Process Explanation**:

2.  \"Rock cycle\" (2010), \"Mass wasting\" (2010), \"Fluvial cycle\"
    (2007).

3.  **Compare/Contrast**:

4.  \"Mediterranean vs. Monsoon climate\" (2007, 2009), \"MDC vs. LDC\"
    (2007).

5.  **Case Studies**:

6.  \"Ocean currents of the Atlantic\" (2009, 2008), \"Deforestation in
    Pakistan\" (2008).

### **Key Takeaways**

1.  **High-Frequency Topics**: Earth's structure, ocean currents,
    climatic regions, and Pakistan's geography.

2.  **Consistent Themes**: Environmental determinism/possibilism,
    urbanization, water conflicts.

3.  **MCQ Focus**: Definitions, founders, and regional facts.

4.  **Essay Focus**: Processes (erosion, tectonics), regional case
    studies (South Asia/Pakistan), and socio-economic impacts.

**Years with Notable Repetition**:

1.  **2008 & 2011**: Plate tectonics, water conflicts.

2.  **2009 & 2010**: Ocean currents, population studies.

3.  **2007 & 2011**: Climate comparisons, urbanization.

issues.
