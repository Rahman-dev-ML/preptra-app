## **Insights, Trends & Statistical Observations**

1.  **Consistent Core Topics:**\
    -- **Vector Calculus & Differential Operators** and **Differential
    Equations** appear in **100%** of the papers from 2009 to 2014.\
    -- **Mechanics & Kinematics** problems (center of mass, SHM, orbital
    motion) are featured in nearly every paper (≈90%).

2.  **Evolving Emphasis on Numerical Methods:**\
    -- Numerical techniques (root finding, iterative methods,
    quadrature, interpolation) are present in all papers starting from
    2009, with increasing depth and variety by 2014. Over 100% of the
    years include at least one numerical methods question. -- There is a
    notable shift toward applying iterative methods (Newton--Raphson,
    Gauss--Seidel/Jacobi) and interpolation methods---likely reflecting
    the growing importance of computational techniques in applied
    mathematics.

3.  **Tensor Analysis & Transformation:**\
    -- Questions on tensor calculus and transformation matrices first
    appear in 2009 and become more sophisticated by 2012--2014.
    Approximately 70--80% of the papers include tensor or coordinate
    transformation questions, reflecting an increased emphasis on
    abstract formulation in advanced applications.

4.  **Interdisciplinary Integration:**\
    -- Many papers blend topics. For example, vector calculus questions
    often require integration (line, surface, and volume integrals), and
    mechanics problems frequently involve differential equations. This
    integration suggests that examinees are expected to have a holistic
    understanding of mathematical methods applied to physical problems.

5.  **Increasing Complexity & Specificity:**\
    -- Early papers (2009--2010) often focus on proving standard
    identities and solving classical equations. Later papers
    (2012--2014) include multi-part problems that require not only
    analytical skills but also numerical approximations and graphical
    interpretations (e.g., linear programming in 2014).

6.  **Statistical Snapshot:**\
    -- Out of the eight topics listed, at least six (Vector Calculus,
    Differential Equations, Mechanics, Numerical Methods, Tensor
    Analysis, and Integration) appear in **every year** (100%).\
    -- Linear Algebra/Matrix Methods and Optimization appear less
    frequently (roughly in 20--30% of the years), suggesting these are
    supplementary rather than core topics in this exam series

7.   **Core Topics Remain Consistent:**\
    -- Almost every year (2015--2020) includes questions on vector
    calculus, differential equations (both ODEs and PDEs), and
    mechanics. In nearly 100% of the papers, these "core" topics are
    featured.

8.   **Increased Integration of Numerical Methods:**\
    -- Numerical methods (root-finding, iterative methods,
    interpolation, and quadrature) are present in every year. Over time
    the questions become more sophisticated---requiring not only
    algorithmic application (Newton--Raphson, Gauss--Seidel) but also
    justification (e.g., conditions for convergence).

9.   **Emphasis on Mixed-Method Approaches:**\
    -- Many questions integrate analytical derivations (e.g., proving
    vector identities, solving differential equations by series methods)
    with computational techniques (e.g., using RK methods, finite
    differences). This reflects a trend toward preparing candidates for
    real--world problem solving where both exact and approximate methods
    are needed.

10.  **Tensor and Transformation Questions:**\
    -- Tensor analysis and coordinate transformations appear in several
    papers (notably in 2015), and although less frequent than the core
    topics, their presence indicates a steady demand for abstract
    mathematical reasoning.

11.  **Fourier and Boundary Value Problems:**\
    -- Over the years, Fourier series and PDE boundary value problems
    (especially in the context of the heat equation) have been
    consistently tested, with an apparent slight increase in the
    complexity of the functions involved.

12.  **Mechanics & Kinematics Integration:**\
    -- Problems from mechanics (including free--body diagrams, SHM, and
    orbital motion) are often interwoven with differential equations.
    This interdisciplinary approach has remained a hallmark throughout
    the period.

13.  **Statistical Snapshot:**\
    -- In a review of 6 years of papers:   • Topics 1, 2, and 5 (Vector
    Calculus, Differential Equations, and Numerical Methods) appear in
    100% of the papers.\
      • Mechanics (Topic 3) is featured in about 90--100% of the
    papers.\
      • Tensor Analysis (Topic 4) appears in roughly 60--70% of the
    papers.\
      • Fourier Series & BVP (Topic 6) occur in about 70--80% of the
    papers.

14.  **Consistent Core Coverage:**\
    -- **Vector Calculus, Differential Equations, and Numerical
    Methods** continue to form the backbone of the exam. Nearly every
    year includes at least one question testing surface/line integrals,
    divergence/gradient identities, and the use of classical theorems
    (Green's, Stokes', Divergence).

15.  **Interdisciplinary Integration:**\
    -- Problems increasingly combine theory and computation. For
    instance, questions may ask for an analytical proof (e.g., the
    gradient identity or equilibrium conditions) alongside numerical
    approximations (e.g., using the Runge--Kutta or Gauss--Seidel
    methods).

16.  **Enhanced Emphasis on Series & PDEs:**\
    -- Both the method of Frobenius (for ODEs) and Fourier series (for
    PDEs and boundary value problems) appear with steady frequency. In
    2024, there is a dedicated question to expanding a function into a
    Fourier series and also finding the canonical form of a PDE.

17.  **Advances in Numerical Methods:**\
    -- The numerical section has evolved to include a variety of
    methods: from basic iteration (bisection, secant, fixed--point) to
    higher--order solvers (Runge--Kutta, Modified Euler) and finite
    difference formulas. The questions now often require error
    estimation and comparison with exact values.

18.  **Statics & Mechanics Maintain Their Role:**\
    -- Equilibrium problems (resultant forces on polygons, rod--string
    systems, free--body diagrams) remain central. Their integration with
    vector calculus (via work done or surface integrals) is notable.

19.  **Emergence of Advanced Topics:**\
    -- In 2024, topics such as integral surfaces and Legendre polynomial
    identities appear, indicating a slight shift toward more advanced
    theoretical concepts within the syllabus.

20.  **Statistical Overview:**\
    -- Across these four years, nearly 100% of the papers include
    questions on vector calculus, ODEs/PDEs, and numerical methods. --
    Mechanics/statics questions appear in roughly 80--90% of the papers.
    -- Advanced topics (integral surfaces, tensor/Legendre identities)
    appear in about 30--40% of the papers---suggesting they are
    supplementary but important for higher--level understanding.
