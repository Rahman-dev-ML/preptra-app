# 2004-2010

### Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in Geology Exams (2004--2010)

#### 1. Recurring Themes

- **Mineralogy & Petrology**

  - **Repeated Topics**: Feldspar, Pyroxene, and Phyllosilicate groups;
    Bowen's Reaction Series; igneous/metamorphic rock classification
    (e.g., syenite, eclogite, peridotite).

  - **Years**: 2004 (Q3, Q8), 2005 (Q1), 2009 (MCQs i, iii, vii), 2010
    (MCQ xiv).

- **Structural Geology**

  - **Repeated Topics**: Faults (Chaman Fault, Horst-Graben), folds
    (Chevron folds, Balochistan folds), rock failure theory.

  - **Years**: 2004 (Q5), 2005 (Q2), 2009 (Q8, MCQs xiv--xvii), 2010
    (Q2).

- **Stratigraphy & Paleontology**

  - **Repeated Topics**: Tertiary sequence in Sindh, Salt Range
    Paleozoic/Mesozoic rocks, cephalopod/brachiopod morphology, index
    fossils.

  - **Years**: 2004 (Q4, Q7), 2005 (Q6), 2009 (Q6, Q8), 2010 (MCQ xix).

- **Plate Tectonics & Earthquakes**

  - **Repeated Topics**: Plate boundaries (Indian Plate focus),
    earthquake epicenter location, seismic-resistant structures.

  - **Years**: 2004 (Q2, Q6), 2009 (Q2, MCQs xii--xiii), 2010 (Q8).

- **Applied Geology**

  - **Repeated Topics**: Dam site selection (Mangla/Tarbela silting),
    groundwater (Darcy's Law, aquifers), mineral/hydrocarbon exploration
    (Saindak, Sui gas field).

  - **Years**: 2004 (Q3--Q6), 2009 (Q3--Q6), 2010 (Q3, Q6--Q7).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 2. Compulsory Short Notes

- **Consistent Terms**:

  - **Structural Terms**: Turbidites, Asthenosphere, Cross bedding,
    Submarine fan, Marker bed.

  - **Minerals/Rocks**: Pyroxene, Kaolinite, Chert, Arkose, Garnet.

  - **Processes**: Enfoliation, Birefringence, Lithification,
    Fossilization.

  - **Regional Features**: Chaman Fault, Main Mantle Thrust, Jhlum
    Group.

  - **Years**: 2004 (Q8), 2005 (Q8), 2009 (Q8), 2010 (Q8).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 3. Regional Focus on Pakistan

- **Key Areas**:

  - **Indus Basin**: Reservoir rocks, hydrocarbon fields (Kohat-Potwar
    Basin).

  - **Balochistan**: Volcanic activity, fold types.

  - **Sindh**: Tertiary strata, Thar coal.

  - **Salt Range**: Paleozoic/Mesozoic stratigraphy.

  - **Years**: 2004 (Q4, Q5), 2009 (Q2, Q6), 2010 (MCQ xx).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 4. Repeated Question Types

- **MCQs (2009--2010)**:

  - Bowen's Reaction Series (2009 vi).

  - Metamorphic rock progression (2009 x).

  - Aquifer types (2010 viii--ix).

  - Mineral hardness/chemistry (2009 i--iii).

- **Long Essays**:

  - **Magma & Igneous Rocks**: Origin of magma, differentiation (2004
    Q2, 2009 Q3).

  - **Coal & Energy**: Thar coal problems, future prospects (2004 Q2,
    2009 Q7).

  - **Earthquake Measures**: Seismic zones in Pakistan (2004 Q6, 2009
    MCQs xii).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 5. Evolution of Exam Format

- **2004--2005**: Essay-only format with compulsory short notes.

- **2009--2010**: Introduction of MCQs (Part I) + essays (Part II).

- **Shift in Focus**: Testing factual recall (MCQs) alongside applied
  knowledge (essays).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

### High-Yield Topics for Preparation

1.  **Mineralogy**: Feldspar, pyroxene, and silicate structures.

2.  **Structural Geology**: Faults (Chaman), folds, rock failure.

3.  **Stratigraphy**: Salt Range, Sindh Tertiary sequence.

4.  **Applied Geology**: Dams (Tarbela/Mangla), groundwater (Darcy's
    Law), hydrocarbon exploration.

5.  **Compulsory Terms**: Memorize definitions for recurring short-note
    terms (e.g., Turbidites, Asthenosphere).

# 2016-2020

- **Repeating Questions:**

  - **2016 Q2:** Physical/chemical weathering + role of water.

  - **2020 Q2:** Differentiate weathering, erosion, transportation.

- **Pattern:**

  - Focus on agents of weathering, erosion types, and links to
    sedimentary structures (e.g., 2018 Q4a, 2019 Q2).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

- **Repeating Questions:**

  - **2016 Q3:** Mineral classes (table with formulae, uses).

  - **2017 Q2:** Nesosilicates (structure + olivine properties).

  - **2019 Q3:** Plagioclase series (physical/optical properties).

  - **2020 Q4:** Silicate classification + isomorphism/polymorphism.

- **Pattern:**

  - Emphasis on silicate subclasses (neso-, plagioclase) and economic
    mineral uses.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 3. Plate Tectonics and Structural Geology

- **Repeating Questions:**

  - **2016 Q4:** Himalayas' tectonic framework *(P)*.

  - **2016 Q5:** Metamorphic facies and plate boundaries.

  - **2017 Q3:** Metamorphic facies during subduction.

  - **2018 Q5b:** Plate boundaries and associated features.

  - **2019 Q4:** Pull-apart basins *(P)*.

  - **2020 Q5:** Lineation vs. foliation.

- **Pattern:**

  - Frequent focus on plate boundaries (convergent, transform), regional
    tectonics (Himalayas, Pakistan), and structural features.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 4. Stratigraphy and Sedimentology

- **Repeating Questions:**

  - **2017 Q4:** Fossilization processes.

  - **2018 Q3b:** Lithostratigraphic units.

  - **2019 Q2:** Formation designation + Salt Range *(P)*.

  - **2020 Q3:** Stratigraphic laws + units.

- **Pattern:**

  - Key topics: lithostratigraphy, fossil interpretation, and Pakistan's
    stratigraphy (e.g., Salt Range).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 5. Economic Geology and Resources

- **Repeating Questions:**

  - **2016 Q3/Q6:** Mineral classes + electromagnetic exploration.

  - **2017 Q6:** Oil/gas prospecting methods.

  - **2018 Q7:** Hydrocarbon traps.

  - **2019 Q5/Q6:** Pegmatites + ore exploration.

  - **2020 Q7:** Hydrocarbon source vs. reservoir rocks.

- **Pattern:**

  - Heavy emphasis on mineral/hydrocarbon exploration, classification,
    and Pakistan's resources (e.g., 2018 Q8b, 2020 Q8c).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 6. Geophysics and Exploration Methods

- **Repeating Questions:**

  - **2016 Q6:** Electromagnetic methods.

  - **2018 Q6:** Gravity anomalies.

  - **2019 Q6:** Geophysical methods for ores.

- **Pattern:**

  - Focus on geophysical techniques (gravity, EM) and data
    interpretation.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 7. Natural Hazards

- **Repeating Questions:**

  - **2016 Q7:** Earthquakes/tsunamis.

  - **2017 Q7:** Landslides *(P)*.

  - **2019 Q7:** Volcanic hazards.

- **Pattern:**

  - Hazards linked to tectonics (earthquakes), slope stability
    (landslides), and volcanic activity.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

- **Recurring Topics (2016--2020):**

  - **Polarizing microscope** (2017).

  - **Coal** (2020), **Copper deposits** (2018), **Radioactive

  - **Dam/tunnel site investigations** (2019, 2020).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

### Key Takeaways

1.  **Pakistan-Specific Geology:** Salt Range, Indian Plate tectonics,
    landslides, and mineral deposits are recurring themes.

2.  **High-Frequency Topics:**

    a.  Plate tectonics (every year).

    b.  Mineralogy (2016--2020).

    c.  Hydrocarbon systems (2016--2020).

3.  **Diagrams/Sketches:** Often required (e.g., 2017 Q3, 2018 Q5).

**Advice for Preparation:** Prioritize plate tectonics, mineral
classification, Pakistan's geology, and economic resources. Practice
diagram-based answers for metamorphic facies, structural features, and
stratigraphy.
