# 2016-2019

### **Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in Anthropology Exams (2016--2019)**

#### **1. Core Definitions and Scope of Anthropology**

1.  **Repeating Questions**:

2.  Define anthropology and differentiate it from other social sciences
    (2016 Q2, 2018 Q2).

3.  Discuss the fields/branches of anthropology (2019 Q2).

4.  **Pattern**: Questions on foundational definitions, scope, and
    uniqueness of anthropology recur every 2--3 years.

#### **2. Religion and Evolution**

1.  **Repeating Questions**:

2.  Define religion and its functions; discuss primitive religion (2016
    Q6).

3.  Tylor's evolutionary scheme of religion and animism (2017 Q5).

4.  Compare divine religions with others (2019 Q7).

5.  **Pattern**: Tylor's theories and religion's role in society are
    tested almost annually.

#### **3. Social Change and Applied Anthropology**

1.  **Repeating Questions**:

2.  Socio-cultural barriers to change (2016 Q3).

3.  Applied anthropology in solving contemporary problems (2017 Q3).

4.  Factors responsible for social change in simple societies (2019 Q4).

5.  **Pattern**: Focus on dynamics of change, barriers, and practical
    applications.

#### **4. Research Methodology**

1.  **Repeating Questions**:

2.  Anthropological research techniques; qualitative vs. quantitative
    (2016 Q4).

3.  Objectivity vs. subjectivity in research (2018 Q3).

4.  Technique vs. method vs. methodology (2019 Q5).

5.  **Pattern**: Annual emphasis on research design, tools, and
    epistemological debates.

#### **5. Kinship, Family, and Marriage Systems**

1.  **Repeating Questions**:

2.  Endogamy, exogamy, consanguine/affine relatives (2016 Q8).

3.  Kinship systems and political allegiances (2018 Q7).

4.  Family types (structure, residence, succession) (2019 Q6).

5.  **Pattern**: Kinship terms and family structures appear in short
    notes and essays yearly.

#### **6. Economic Systems**

1.  **Repeating Questions**:

2.  Evolution of economic systems (reciprocity → redistribution →
    market) (2018 Q5).

3.  Primitive economic organization (2019 Q3).

4.  **Pattern**: Economic anthropology, especially evolutionary models,
    recurs every 1--2 years.

#### **7. Contemporary Issues in Pakistan**

1.  **Repeating Questions**:

2.  Contemporary social problems of Pakistan (2016 Q5).

3.  Sectarianism and national integration (2018 Q6).

4.  Poverty vs. population pressure debate (2017 Q7).

5.  **Pattern**: Pakistan-specific societal issues are tested every 1--2
    years.

#### **8. Cultural Concepts**

1.  **Repeating Questions**:

2.  Define culture and its characteristics (2016 Q2).

3.  Cultural relativism (2018 Q8).

4.  Culture responding to human needs (2018 Q4).

5.  **Pattern**: Core cultural theories and relativism are frequent
    themes.

#### **9. Short Notes on Key Terms**

1.  **Repeating Terms**:

2.  **Kinship**: Sororate, virilocal residence (2017, 2018).

3.  **Research Tools**: Participant observation (2018, 2019).

4.  **Cultural Terms**: Ethnocentrism (2016), cultural relativism
    (2018).

5.  **Pattern**: 4--5 short notes per year, often overlapping terms
    across years.

#### **10. Theories and Theorists**

1.  **Repeating Topics**:

2.  Morgan & Tylor's social evolution (2016 Q7).

3.  Tylor's animism (2017 Q5).

4.  **Pattern**: Classic 19th-century evolutionary theorists are
    frequently referenced.

### **Key Takeaways**

1.  **High-Frequency Topics**: Religion (Tylor), kinship systems,
    research methods, Pakistan's social issues, and cultural
    definitions.

2.  **Recurring Formats**: Short notes on terminology, comparative
    essays (e.g., qualitative vs. quantitative), and application-based
    questions (e.g., solving global issues through anthropology).

3.  **Theoretical Focus**: Evolutionary theories (Morgan, Tylor) and
    debates on objectivity in research.

**Years with Overlapping Questions**:

1.  **2016 & 2018**: Anthropology's definition vs. social sciences.

2.  **2016 & 2017**: Tylor's theories on religion.

3.  **2017 & 2019**: Economic systems and kinship terms.
