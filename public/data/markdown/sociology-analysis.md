**Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in Sociology Exams
(2000--2005)**

**1. Theoretical Foundations**

1.  **Key Theorists**:

2.  **Karl Marx**: Division of labor, class conflict, alienation,
    capitalism (2001--2005).

3.  **Émile Durkheim**: Suicide, mechanical/organic solidarity (2001,
    2003, 2004).

4.  **Max Weber**: Bureaucracy, Calvinism and capitalism (2002--2005).

5.  **Ibn Khaldun**: Contributions as \"Father of Sociology\" (2001,
    2004).

6.  **Repeating Questions**:

7.  \"Compare Marx and Durkheim's views on division of labor\" (2002).

8.  \"Discuss Weber's advocacy of bureaucracy\" (2002).

9.  \"Critique Durkheim's theory of suicide\" (2003).

**2. Social Issues in Pakistan**

1.  **Core Topics**:

2.  Feudal system impact (2001, 2000, 2004).

3.  Juvenile delinquency (2002, 2003).

4.  Poverty alleviation strategies (2004, 2005).

5.  Urbanization challenges (2000, 2006).

6.  **Repeating Questions**:

7.  \"Impact of the feudal system in Pakistan\" (2001, 2000).

8.  \"Factors behind juvenile delinquency in Pakistan\" (2002, 2003).

9.  \"Government/NGO roles in poverty reduction\" (2004).

**3. Research Methodology**

1.  **Key Areas**:

2.  Experimental vs. survey methods (2002, 2003).

3.  Sampling techniques (stratified random, probability/non-probability)
    (2004, 2005).

4.  Variables (nominal, ordinal, ratio) (2003--2005).

5.  **Repeating Questions**:

6.  \"When is survey research appropriate?\" (2003).

7.  \"Advantages of experimental methods\" (2002).

8.  \"Define stratified random sampling\" (MCQs in 2004, 2005).

**4. Culture and Socialization**

1.  **Core Topics**:

2.  Role of culture in socialization (2001, 2000).

3.  Social control mechanisms (2001, 2005).

4.  Cultural lag (2000, 2004).

5.  **Repeating Questions**:

6.  \"How does culture shape socialization?\" (2001).

7.  \"Internal/external means of social control in Pakistan\" (2001).

8.  \"Define cultural lag\" (MCQs in 2000, 2004).

**5. Demography and Population**

1.  **Key Areas**:

2.  Population growth rate (2003--2005).

3.  Fertility/mortality trends (2004, 2005).

4.  Migration (internal and international) (2001, 2000).

5.  **Repeating Questions**:

6.  \"Factors influencing Pakistan's population growth\" (2003, 2005).

7.  \"Impact of high population growth\" (2000, 2005).

8.  \"Define emigration\" (MCQs in 2002, 2004).

**6. Recurring MCQ Themes**

1.  **Definitions**:

2.  Affinal kin, culture complex, ethnography (2001--2005).

3.  **Theorist Attribution**:

4.  \"Who coined *alienation*?\" (Marx, 2000, 2002).

5.  \"Founder of Sociology?\" (Ibn Khaldun, 2004, 2005).

6.  **Demographic Data**:

7.  Pakistan's population doubling time (2002), poverty rate (2004,
    2005).

8.  **Social Institutions**:

9.  Family as primary socialization agent (2003, 2005).

**Year-Specific Highlights**

1.  **2001**: Focus on socialization, Ibn Khaldun, feudal system.

2.  **2002**: Emphasis on Marx vs. Durkheim, experimental methods.

3.  **2003**: Durkheim's suicide theory, juvenile delinquency.

4.  **2004**: Poverty strategies, religion's role in social tension.

5.  **2005**: Social research steps, race/racism, status determination.

6.  **2000**: Cultural change, social mobility, feudalism critique.

This analysis shows consistent testing of classical theories,
Pakistan-specific social issues, and methodological rigor across years.
Students should prioritize mastering Marx/Durkheim/Weber, Pakistan's
social challenges, and research terminology.
