# 2001-2005

### **Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in Law Exams (2001--2005)**

**Note:** Questions are categorized by recurring themes and repeated
topics across years.

#### **1. Civil Procedure Code (CPC)**

**Key Patterns:**

1.  **Res Judicata vs. Res Sub Judice**

2.  *2001 Q1*: Essentials and distinction between Res Judicata and Res
    Sub Judice.

3.  *2002 Q3*: Rule of Res Sub Judice (pending adjudication).

4.  *2004 Q1*: Universal application of Res Judicata.

5.  *2005 Q3*: Distinction between Res Judicata and Res Sub Judice.

6.  **Pleadings and Procedural Remedies**

7.  *2003 Q1*: Doctrine of Restitution.

8.  *2005 Q1*: Rules for pleadings.

9.  *2001 Q3*: Representative suits.

10. *2004 Q2*: Decree execution challenges.

11. **Compulsory Questions (Procedural Details)**

12. Interpleader suits (2001 Q10, 2005 Q2).

13. Attachment of property (2004 Q6).

14. Inherent powers of courts (2003 Q2).

#### **2. Contract Act**

**Key Patterns:**

1.  **Void vs. Voidable Contracts**

2.  *2001 Q4*: Define and distinguish.

3.  *2002 Q4*: Quasi contracts.

4.  *2003 Q4*: Jurisdiction-ousting agreements.

5.  *2004 Q4*: Fraud vitiating contracts.

6.  **Contract Essentials**

7.  *2005 Q4*: Essentials of a valid contract.

8.  *2003 Q5*: Consideration and guarantee.

9.  *2001 Q5*: Undue influence (e.g., parda-nashin ladies).

10. **Specific Contracts**

11. Bailment (2005 Q5).

12. Tender (2002 Q13, 2005 Q5).

13. Impossibility of performance (2004 Q6).

#### **3. Law of Torts**

**Key Patterns:**

1.  **Negligence and Defenses**

2.  *2001 Q8*: Onus of proof in negligence.

3.  *2002 Q8*: Battery vs. Assault.

4.  *2005 Q8*: Absolute liability and statutory negligence.

5.  *2004 Q7*: Defenses in tort (e.g., statutory authority).

6.  **Defamation**

7.  *2001 Q9*: Libel vs. Slander.

8.  *2005 Q19*: Written vs. spoken defamation.

9.  **Vicarious Liability**

10. *2003 Q8*: Vicarious liability.

11. *2004 Q18*: Employer liability for contractors.

#### **4. Pakistan Penal Code (PPC)**

**Key Patterns:**

1.  **Mens Rea and General Exceptions**

2.  *2001 Q3*: Mens Rea in criminal liability.

3.  *2002 Q7*: Stages of offense commission.

4.  *2005 Q3*: General exceptions (e.g., self-defense).

5.  **Homicide and Hurt**

6.  *2001 Q2*: Culpable homicide vs. murder.

7.  *2002 Q8*: Qisas and Diyat.

8.  *2005 Q2*: Injury and robbery definitions.

9.  **Specific Offenses**

10. Cheating (2004 Q8).

11. Forgery (2003 Q15).

12. Criminal intimidation (2003 Q8).

#### **5. Criminal Procedure Code (CrPC)**

**Key Patterns:**

1.  **FIR vs. Complaint**

2.  *2002 Q4*: Differences.

3.  *2005 Q5*: FIR and retracted confession.

4.  **Bail and Arrest**

5.  *2002 Q6*: Bail in bailable/non-bailable cases.

6.  *2005 Q4*: Arrest without warrant.

7.  **Trial Procedures**

8.  Summary trials (2002 Q5).

9.  Revision vs. Appeal (2003 Q5).

#### **6. Qanun-e-Shahadat Order (QSO)**

**Key Patterns:**

1.  **Evidence Types**

2.  *2001 Q7*: Oral vs. documentary evidence.

3.  *2002 Q7*: Hearsay and primary/secondary evidence.

4.  *2005 Q9*: Competent witnesses and estoppel.

5.  **Burden of Proof**

6.  *2004 Q3*: Shahada ala al Shahada.

7.  *2005 Q7*: Burden of proof in civil vs. criminal cases.

#### **Compulsory Questions (Common Themes)**

1.  **Procedural Law**

2.  Appealability of orders (2001 Q10, 2002 Q10).

3.  True/False on Res Judicata (2001 Q10).

4.  Void vs. Voidable agreements (2001 Q16--18).

5.  **Contract Act**

6.  Validity of minor contracts (2004 Q13).

7.  Consideration (2003 Q11).

8.  **Tort Defenses**

9.  Damnum Sine Injuria (2004 Q17).

10. Contributory negligence (2004 Q16).

### **Conclusion**

1.  **Core Repeating Topics:**Res Judicata, Void Contracts, Negligence,
    Mens Rea, FIR vs. Complaint.

2.  **High-Weightage Areas:**CPC (Res Judicata, Pleadings), Contract Act
    (Essentials), PPC (Homicide), CrPC (Bail).

3.  **Exam Strategy:**Focus on distinctions (e.g., Res Judicata vs. Res
    Sub Judice) and procedural nuances (appeals, evidence).

**Years with Notable Repetitions:**

1.  **2001--2002:** Heavy emphasis on Res Judicata and Contract
    distinctions.

2.  **2003--2005:** Shift toward procedural law (CrPC, QSO) and tort
    defenses.

    and **Contract Act fraud**.

### **Conclusion**

and essay questions requiring **case law references** and **distinctions
between concepts**. Key areas to prioritize:

1.  **CPC**: Res judicata, execution of decrees.

2.  **Contract Act**: Minor agreements, coercion/undue influence.

3.  **Torts**: Defamation, strict liability.

4.  **CrPC/PPC**: FIR vs. complaint, qisas/diyat.

5.  **Evidence Law**: Confessions, estoppel.
