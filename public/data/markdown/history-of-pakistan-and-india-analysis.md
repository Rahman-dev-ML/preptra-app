# 2000-2005

### Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in History of Pakistan and India Exams (2000--2005)

#### 1. Mughal Empire (Repeated Topics)

- **Architecture**:

  - \"Distinctive features of Mughal architecture\" (2000, Q1; 2002,
    Q7b; 2004, Q6).

  - Specific structures: Taj Mahal, Buland Darwaza, Moti Masjid.

- **Administrative Policies**:

  - Sher Shah Suri's reforms (2000, Q6; 2002, Q4; 2004, Q4).

  - Akbar's Rajput policy and Din-i-Ilahi (2000, Q4; 2002, Q5; 2004,
    Q5).

  - Mansabdari System (2004, Q7b).

- **Decline of the Mughals**:

  - Role of nobility and foreign invasions (2002, Q6; 2003, Q1).

  - Aurangzeb's reign (2003, Q6; 2005, Q6).

#### 2. Delhi Sultanate (Repeated Topics)

- **Rulers and Policies**:

  - Alauddin Khalji's price control and fiscal reforms (2001, Q5; 2003,
    Q2; 2005, Q3).

  - Muhammad bin Tughluq's contradictory policies (2000, Q7; 2002, Q2;
    2003, Q3).

  - Balban's consolidation strategies (2004, Q2).

- **Slave Dynasty**:

  - Strengths and weaknesses of the slave system (2001, Q2).

#### 3. Indo-Islamic Culture and Society

- **Cultural Synthesis**:

  - Impact of Hindu-Muslim contact under the Sultanate (2004, Q3).

  - Literary contributions (e.g., Amir Khusrau, Ain-i-Akbari) (2001,
    Q7a; 2002, Q7a; 2005, Q7).

- **Art and Architecture**:

  - Mughal painting and Indo-Islamic art (2002, Q7b; 2005, Q7d).

#### 4. Colonial and Post-Colonial History (Paper II Focus)

- **Two-Nation Theory**:

  - Role of Sir Syed Ahmad Khan (2000, Q1; 2003, Q5; 2005, Q1).

  - Allama Iqbal's 1930 address (2004, Q4).

- **Key Movements/Events**:

  - Partition of Bengal (1905) (2000, Q2; 2004, Q2).

  - Khilafat Movement (2000, Q3; 2003, Q3).

  - Lucknow Pact (2002, Q3; 2004, Q2).

  - Cabinet Mission Plan (2002, Q4).

- **Constitutional Development in Pakistan**:

  - Delays in constitution-making (2000, Q6; 2002, Q5; 2003, Q8).

  - 1956/1973 Constitutions (2005, Q7a).

#### 5. Pakistan's Early Challenges

- **Separation of East Pakistan**:

  - Causes and effects (2000, Q7b; 2002, Q6; 2004, Q6).

- **Foreign Relations**:

  - Relations with India (Kashmir issue) (2000, Q7d; 2004, Q7; 2005,
    Q6).

  - Role in OIC (2002, Q7).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

### Compulsory Questions (MCQs/Fill-in-the-Blanks Trends)

1.  **Dates and Reigns**:

    a.  Battle of Panipat (1556, 1526), Sher Shah's rule (1539--1545),
        Akbar's death (1605).

    b.  Example: *\"Sher Shah proclaimed king in: (a) 1539\"* (2000,
        Q19).

2.  **Historical Figures**:

    a.  Authors (e.g., *Akbar Nama* by Abul Fazal, *Reconstruction of
        Religious Thought* by Iqbal).

    b.  Founders (e.g., Bahmani Kingdom by Ala-ud-Din Bahman Shah).

3.  **Treaties and Agreements**:

    a.  Indus Basin Treaty (1960), Simla Agreement (1972).

4.  **Constitutional Milestones**:

    a.  Objective Resolution (1949), 8th Amendment (1985).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

### Year-Specific Highlights

- **2000**: Focus on Mughal judicial systems, Sultanate-Ulama relations.

- **2001**: Emphasis on Sultan Muhammad Ghori's campaigns.

- **2002**: Analysis of Sher Shah's reforms and Mughal decline.

- **2003**: Aurangzeb's rule and British colonial influence.

- **2004**: Arab conquest's impact, Balban's consolidation.

- **2005**: Muslim cultural contributions (cuisine, architecture).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

### Key Takeaways

- **Repeated Essay Themes**: Mughal governance, Sultanate reforms,
  Two-Nation Theory, constitutional challenges.

- **MCQs**: Focus on factual recall (dates, treaties, authors).

- **Consistent Structure**: Compulsory questions test precision; essays
  demand analytical depth on recurring historical arcs.
