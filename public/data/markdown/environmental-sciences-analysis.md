**Exam Preparation Recommendations**

1.  **Policy & Global Frameworks:**

    - Master the evolution and significance of international treaties
      and national policies. Know the landmark events (Rio Summit,
      UNFCCC, Kyoto, Agenda 21) and their implications.

2.  **Climate Change & Mitigation:**

    - Understand the mechanisms of climate change, including greenhouse
      gases and the technological as well as natural drivers.

    - Be prepared to suggest and evaluate adaptation and mitigation
      strategies, particularly in a local context.

3.  **Waste Management & Pollution Control:**

    - Review the classification, handling, and disposal methods for
      hazardous waste, wastewater, and solid waste.

    - Familiarize yourself with air pollution control measures,
      including smog management and atmospheric cleaning techniques.

4.  **Environmental Technologies:**

    - Study the principles of bioremediation, anaerobic treatment for
      biogas, and alternative energy resources.

    - Understand the role and outcomes of Clean Development Mechanisms.

5.  **Process & Participation:**

    - Focus on the steps and importance of Environmental Impact
      Assessments, along with the role of public participation and
      baseline data collection.

6.  **Short Notes & Definitions:**

    - Prepare brief, precise notes on recurring topics (e.g., CDM,
      eutrophication, GIS, wetlands) to quickly revise essential
      concepts
