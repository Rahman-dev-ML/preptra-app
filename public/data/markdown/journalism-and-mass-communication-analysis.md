# 2006-2010

### **Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in CSS Journalism Exams (2006--2010)**

#### **1. Mass Communication**

1.  **Pattern**: Definitions, process, importance, barriers, and
    relevance in the modern age.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  2006 Q1: *\"What do you know about Mass Communication? Describe its
    importance in the present age.\"*

4.  2008 Q2: *\"Discuss the concept and process of communication\...
    importance of Mass Communication.\"*

5.  2010 Q2: *\"Mass Communication is considered important in modern
    age. Discuss the concept and process.\"*

#### **2. News**

1.  **Pattern**: Functions, values, components, societal impact, and
    differentiation from related concepts.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  2006 Q2: *\"Explain what is news, its functions and values.\"*

4.  2008 Q3: *\"'News' is the real soul of Journalism\... major
    functions and values.\"*

5.  2010 Q4: *\"Describe news 'which is the real soul of journalism'
    with examples.\"*

#### **3. Role of Media in Pakistan**

1.  **Pattern**: Focus on historical and contemporary roles of
    print/electronic media, challenges, and social impact.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  2007 Q2: *\"Give your evaluation of the role of print journalism in
    Pakistan.\"*

4.  2008 Q4: *\"Critically evaluate the role of present TV Channels in
    social development.\"*

5.  2009 Q6: *\"How 'Print Media' is important after the development of
    'Electronic Media'?\"*

#### **4. Historical Aspects (Muslim Press & Pakistan Movement)**

1.  **Pattern**: Role of Muslim Press during the Pakistan Movement, key
    figures, and challenges.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  2006 Q4: *\"Explain the difficulties faced by the Muslim Press
    during the movement of Pakistan.\"*

4.  2008 Q5: *\"Discuss the role played by the Muslim Press during
    Pakistan Movement.\"*

5.  2010 Q5: *\"Critically examine the role of Muslim press during the
    Pakistan Movement.\"*

#### **5. Public Relations & Advertising**

1.  **Pattern**: Definitions, roles, principles, trends, and
    organizational impact.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  2006 Q5/Q6: *\"Public Relations is actually Image Building
    activity.\"*

4.  2008 Q7: *\"Define 'Advertising'\... principles of successful
    advertising.\"*

5.  2009 Q8: *\"Difference between PRO and Personal Assistant\...
    responsibilities of a good PRO.\"*

#### **6. Journalism Terms (MCQs)**

1.  **Pattern**: Repeated testing of basic journalism terminology,
    historical firsts, and abbreviations.

2.  **Repeating MCQ Topics**:

3.  **ABC**: Audit Bureau of Circulation (2006, 2008, 2010).

4.  **Byline**: Reporter's name with story (2006, 2008, 2010).

5.  **Press Laws**: 1963 Press Ordinance (2006, 2008, 2010).

6.  **Yellow Journalism**: Sensationalism (2006, 2009, 2010).

#### **7. Press Freedom & Ethics**

1.  **Pattern**: Debates on freedom vs. responsibility, press codes, and
    global comparisons.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  2008 Q9: *\"'Freedom of Press' and 'Responsibility'\... evaluate the
    case of Pakistan.\"*

4.  2009 Q9: *\"Describe an ideal 'Press Code of Ethics' for Pakistani
    journalists.\"*

5.  2010 Q9: *\"Discuss 'Free Press' and 'Responsibility' in
    developing/developed societies.\"*

#### **8. Electronic Media Trends**

1.  **Pattern**: Growth, challenges, and societal impact of TV channels.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  2007 Q3: *\"Survey the development of electronic media in
    Pakistan.\"*

4.  2009 Q5: *\"Evaluate critically the performance of private TV
    channels in Pakistan.\"*

### **Key Takeaways**

1.  **High-Frequency Topics**: Mass Communication, News, Muslim Press,
    PR/Advertising, and Media Ethics.

2.  **MCQ Focus**: Definitions (e.g., *embargo*, *byline*), historical
    firsts (e.g., first Urdu newspaper), and laws (e.g., PEMRA).

3.  **Consistent Structure**: Compulsory MCQs + Essay sections split
    into theory and applied questions.

**Years with Overlapping Content**:

1.  **2006--2008**: Heavy focus on foundational concepts.

2.  **2009--2010**: Shift toward ethics, globalization, and modern media
    challenges.

Candidates should prioritize mastering recurring themes and terminology
while practicing analytical writing on media roles and ethics.

    journalism (2012 Q8, 2013 Q8).

11. **Practical Applications**

    Q2), press releases (2013 Q9).

1.  **Media Theories**

2.  2011 Qxv (McLuhan), 2013 Q1, 2014 Q3 (agenda setting), 2015 Q4.

### **Strategic Preparation Tips**

1.  **Focus on Theorists**: McLuhan, Schramm, agenda-setting/framing
    theories.

2.  **Ethical Debates**: Freedom vs regulation, PR ethics, online
    journalism challenges.

3.  **Technology Trends**: Social media, internet's cultural impact,
    decline of traditional media.

4.  **Historical Context**: Key figures (Zafar Ali Khan), Urdu
    journalism milestones.

5.  **Current Affairs**: Link past questions to recent events (e.g.,
    media's role in 2024 elections).

This analysis highlights the exam's emphasis on theoretical
understanding, ethical dilemmas, technological shifts, and historical
context---key areas for focused revision.
