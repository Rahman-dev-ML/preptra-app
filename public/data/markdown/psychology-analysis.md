# 2001-2005

### **Underlying Patterns & Repeating Questions in FPSC Psychology Exams (2001--2005)**

Below are the recurring themes, topics, and questions across Papers I
and II, categorized by subject area with years specified:

### **1. Schools/Systems of Psychology**

1.  **Paper I**:

2.  **2001 Q1**: Critically evaluate different schools of psychology.

3.  **2003 Q1**: Structuralism vs. recent trends.

4.  **2004 Q1**: Aims/methods of Structuralism vs. Functionalism.

5.  **2005 Q6**: Recent trends in Behaviorism.

6.  **Pattern**: Focus on Structuralism, Functionalism, and Behaviorism.

### **2. Nervous System & Biological Bases**

1.  **Paper I**:

2.  **2001 Q2**: Central vs. Peripheral Nervous System (CNS/PNS).

3.  **2003 Q2**: Role of the Peripheral Nervous System.

4.  **2004 Q2**: Subdivisions of the brain.

5.  **MCQs**: Autonomic Nervous System (ANS) (2001, 2004, 2005),
    neurotransmitters (2004), thalamus/hypothalamus (2001, 2003).

### **3. Learning Theories**

1.  **Paper I**:

2.  **2001 Q3**: Define learning + reinforcement views.

3.  **2003 Q3**: Parameters of conditioning.

4.  **2004 Q3**: Operant vs. Classical conditioning.

5.  **MCQs**: Thorndike's Law of Effect (2004), Pavlovian CR (2005),
    cognitive maps (Tolman, 2004).

### **4. Developmental Psychology**

1.  **Paper II**:

2.  **2001 Q2**: Physical/social development post-birth.

3.  **2002 Q4**: Piaget's cognitive stages.

4.  **2003 Q1**: Infant behavior study methods.

5.  **2004 Q6**: Piaget's stages.

6.  **MCQs**: Object permanence (2002, 2004), Erikson's identity vs.
    role confusion (2002, 2005), longitudinal vs. cross-sectional
    methods (2002).

### **5. Personality & Assessment**

1.  **Paper I**:

2.  **2001 Q7**: Nature of groups (social loafing/riots).

3.  **2004 Q6**: Personality tests (types).

4.  **MCQs**: Trait theory (2001), projective tests (TAT/Rorschach,
    2004, 2005), Jung's introversion/extroversion (2003, 2005).

5.  **Paper II**:

6.  **2001 Q4**: Measuring Pakistani personality.

7.  **2003 Q3**: Clinical diagnosis sources.

### **6. Motivation & Emotion**

1.  **Paper I**:

2.  **2001 Q4**: Biogenic vs. sociogenic motives.

3.  **2005 Q1**: Motivational factors in learning.

4.  **MCQs**: Maslow's hierarchy (2001, 2004), homeostasis (2001, 2003,
    2005), Cannon-Bard theory (2003).

### **7. Perception**

1.  **Paper I**:

2.  **2001 Q5**: Perceptual process.

3.  **2003 Q5**: Determinants of perception.

4.  **2004 Q7**: Perceptual constancy.

5.  **MCQs**: Depth perception (2001), monocular cues (2005), Gestalt
    principles (2005).

### **8. Clinical Psychology & Disorders**

1.  **Paper II**:

2.  **2001 Q5**: Schizophrenia theories.

3.  **2003 Q7**: Psychosomatic disorders vs. psychosis.

4.  **2005 Q6**: Psychoneurosis vs. psychosis.

5.  **MCQs**: Systematic desensitization (2001, 2003), learned
    helplessness (2002), Down's syndrome (2001, 2002).

### **9. Social Psychology**

1.  **Paper I**:

2.  **2001 Q6**: Prejudice theories + reduction techniques.

3.  **2005 Q7**: Group dynamics in tension diffusion.

4.  **MCQs**: Cognitive dissonance (Festinger, 2001, 2004), social
    loafing (2001, 2005).

### **10. Intelligence & Testing**

1.  **Paper II**:

2.  **2001 Q6**: IQ vs. EQ.

3.  **2004 Q4**: Guilford's Structure of Intellect.

4.  **MCQs**: Binet-Simon Scale (2002, 2005), fluid vs. crystallized
    intelligence (2003).

### **Compulsory MCQ Repeats (Question 8)**

1.  **Nervous System**: ANS (2001, 2004, 2005), thalamus (2001).

2.  **Theorists**: Maslow (2001, 2004), Jung (2003, 2005), Festinger
    (2001).

3.  **Disorders**: Down's syndrome (2001, 2002), psychosomatic disorders
    (2003, 2005).

4.  **Learning**: Reinforcement (2001, 2004), cognitive maps (2004).

5.  **Developmental**: Object permanence (2002, 2004), Erikson's stages
    (2002, 2005).

### **Key Takeaways**

1.  **High-Weightage Topics**:

2.  Piaget's cognitive development (2001--2005).

3.  Nervous system (CNS/PNS, ANS).

4.  Personality assessment tools (TAT, MMPI).

5.  Maslow's hierarchy and self-actualization.

6.  Clinical disorders (schizophrenia, depression).

7.  **Frequent Theorists**:

8.  **Piaget** (developmental stages).

9.  **Freud** (defense mechanisms, personality).

10. **Bandura** (social learning).

11. **Skinner** (operant conditioning).

12. **MCQ Patterns**:

13. Definitions (e.g., homeostasis, reinforcement).

14. Matching theorists to concepts (e.g., Festinger â†’ cognitive
    dissonance).

15. Developmental milestones (e.g., sensorimotor stage).

Use this analysis to prioritize study areas and practice past papers for
recurring themes!

#### **4. Year-Specific Trends**

1.  **2009**: Heavy focus on schools of psychology (Structuralism,
    Functionalism) and Freudian concepts.

2.  **2010**: Emphasis on developmental stages (Piaget) and clinical
    therapies (CBT, REBT).

3.  **2011**: Detailed questions on perception (sensation vs.
    perception) and intelligence testing.

4.  **2012**: Repeated essays on classical conditioning and job
    satisfaction.

5.  **2013**: Integration of biological bases (neurotransmitters, brain
    lobes) with applied topics (attitudes, group norms).

#### **Key Takeaways for Preparation:**

1.  **Master Foundational Theories**: Freud, Piaget, Skinner, and Maslow
    are tested annually.

2.  **Compare & Contrast**: Schools of psychology, conditioning types,
    and developmental stages.

3.  **Focus on Applications**: Job satisfaction, DSM classification, and
    therapy techniques.

4.  **Ethics & Research Methods**: MCQs often test variables, ethics,
    and statistical terms.

This analysis highlights the need to prioritize recurring themes while
ensuring familiarity with evolving topics like newer therapies (e.g.,
REBT in 2013).

# 2014-2018

### **Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in Psychology Exams (2014--2018)**

#### **1. Major Psychological Perspectives/Theories**

1.  **Years/Questions**:

2.  2014 (Paper I, Q2): Major perspectives of psychology.

3.  2015 (Paper I, Q2): Schools of psychology (e.g., behavioral,
    cognitive).

4.  2017 (Paper I, Q2): Personality theories (psychoanalytic, trait,
    humanistic).

5.  **Pattern**: Focus on explaining foundational schools (behaviorism,
    cognitive, humanistic) and comparing their relevance.

#### **2. Conditioning (Classical vs Operant)**

1.  **Years/Questions**:

2.  2014 (Paper I, Q3): Differentiation between classical and operant
    conditioning.

3.  2015 (Paper I, Q4): Define learning and differentiate conditioning
    types.

4.  2016 (Paper I, Q3): Role of reinforcement/extinction in classical
    conditioning.

5.  2018 (Paper I, Q3, Q8iii): Associative learning and reinforcer
    roles.

6.  **Pattern**: Repeated emphasis on distinguishing classical (Pavlov)
    vs operant (Skinner) conditioning, with applications.

#### **3. Personality Theories and Assessment**

1.  **Years/Questions**:

2.  2014 (Paper I, Q4): Theories (Freud, Maslow) and tests (objective vs
    projective).

3.  2015 (Paper II, Q5): Ethics of personality testing.

4.  2016 (Paper I, Q4): Theoretical perspectives and assessment
    techniques.

5.  2017 (Paper I, Q2): Major personality theories.

6.  **Pattern**: Frequent focus on psychoanalytic, trait, and humanistic
    theories, alongside tests like MMPI, Rorschach.

#### **4. Child Development Theories (Piaget & Vygotsky)**

1.  **Years/Questions**:

2.  2014 (Paper II, Q3): Piaget's cognitive development theory.

3.  2015 (Paper II, Q7): Piaget's Cognitive-Stage Theory.

4.  2015 (Paper II, Q2): Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development.

5.  2016 (Paper I, Q5): Physical, cognitive, social development.

6.  2017 (Paper I, Q8C): Development in childhood.

7.  **Pattern**: Piaget's stages and Vygotsky's sociocultural approach
    are recurring themes.

#### **5. Brain Structure and Function**

1.  **Years/Questions**:

2.  2014 (Paper I, Q7): Lobes and their functions.

3.  2015 (Paper I, Q3): Brain parts and neuron functions.

4.  2016 (Paper I, Q2): Nervous system and endocrine system roles.

5.  **Pattern**: Questions on lobes (frontal, temporal), neurons, and
    endocrine glands (e.g., pituitary).

#### **6. Abnormal Behavior and Therapies**

1.  **Years/Questions**:

2.  2014 (Paper II, Q4): Defining abnormal behavior and treatment
    approaches.

3.  2016 (Paper I, Q6): Anxiety/personality disorder treatments.

4.  2017 (Paper I, Q6): Psychological disorders and therapies (CBT,
    psychoanalysis).

5.  2018 (Paper I, Q6): Humanistic therapy characteristics.

6.  **Pattern**: Focus on definitions, contrasting perspectives
    (behavioral vs cognitive), and therapeutic techniques.

#### **7. Intelligence (IQ vs EQ)**

1.  **Years/Questions**:

2.  2014 (Paper II, Q7): Traditional vs emotional intelligence.

3.  2015 (Paper I, Q5): EQ importance for professionals.

4.  2017 (Paper I, Q5): Theories of emotional intelligence (Goleman).

5.  **Pattern**: Emphasis on EQ's role in leadership and social skills.

#### **8. Socialization and Group Dynamics**

1.  **Years/Questions**:

2.  2014 (Paper I, Q8a): Agents of socialization.

3.  2015 (Paper I, Q7): Process of socialization.

4.  2016 (Paper I, Q8c): Group dynamics.

5.  2018 (Paper I, Q7): National stereotypes and social harmony.

6.  **Pattern**: Topics like family, peers, media as socialization
    agents, and group norms.

#### **9. Psychological Testing**

1.  **Years/Questions**:

2.  2014 (Paper I, Q4): Objective vs projective tests.

3.  2015 (Paper II, Q4): Psychological Testing Movement.

4.  2017 (Paper I, Q4): Reliability/types of psychological tests.

5.  2017 (Paper I, Q8B): Test validity.

6.  **Pattern**: Focus on test reliability, validity, and ethical
    considerations.

#### **10. Leadership Styles**

1.  **Years/Questions**:

2.  2016 (Paper I, Q7): Leadership styles and decision-making.

3.  2017 (Paper I, Q7): Transformational vs transactional leadership.

4.  **Pattern**: Comparison of leadership theories with real-world
    examples.

### **Most Frequently Repeated Topics**

1.  **Conditioning** (4 years).

2.  **Personality Theories** (4 years).

3.  **Piaget/Vygotsky's Developmental Theories** (3 years).

4.  **Brain Structure** (3 years).

5.  **IQ vs EQ** (3 years).

### **Notable Trends**

1.  **Application-Based Questions**: Linking theories to real-world
    issues (e.g., terrorism in 2014, national stereotypes in 2018).

2.  **Short Notes**: Common topics: endocrine glands (2014, 2015), group
    dynamics (2014, 2016), juvenile delinquency (2014, 2016).

3.  **Shift Toward Modern Issues**: By 2018, questions on the \"role of
    the mind\" and social harmony reflect contemporary debates.

Candidates should prioritize mastering foundational theories
(conditioning, personality, development), brain functions, testing
methodologies, and applications to societal issues.

### **Key Observations:**

1.  **High-Frequency Topics**: Emotion theories, personality,
    intelligence, testing ethics, and disorders are tested almost every
    year.

2.  **Theoretical Comparisons**: Questions often ask to \"compare and
    contrast\" theories (e.g., James-Lange vs. Cannon-Bard) or
    approaches (e.g., Kohlberg vs. Gilligan).

3.  **Applied Focus**: Testing methods (projective vs. objective),
    therapeutic techniques (CBT, psychoanalysis), and ethics are
    emphasized.

4.  **Repetition of Psychologists**: Piaget, Freud, Gardner, Kohlberg,
    Milgram, and Gestalt theorists are recurring names.

**Advice for Preparation**: Focus on core theories, practice
comparisons, and understand DSM criteria for disorders. Revise testing
ethics and key experiments (e.g., Milgram).
