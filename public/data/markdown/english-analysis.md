# 2000-2004

Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in CSS English Past Papers
(2000--2004)

1\. Précis Writing

Pattern: Summarize a passage (\~1/3 length) and suggest a suitable
title.

Repeats: Every year (mandatory question).

2\. Comprehension Passages

Pattern: Read a passage and answer questions (inference, vocabulary,
context).

Repeating Elements:

Inferential Questions: E.g., \"What do you infer about the Troubles?\"
(2003).

Vocabulary Explanations: Define underlined words/phrases (e.g.,
\"dubbed,\" \"was sacked\").

Title Suggestion: E.g., \"Suggest a title for the passage\" (2002).

3\. Essay/Comprehensive Note

Pattern: Write 250--300 words on one of 3--4 given topics (proverbs,
philosophical quotes, social issues).

Repeating Themes:

Proverbs/Quotes: E.g., \"Gather ye rosebuds while ye may\" (2004),
\"Crisis tests the true mettle of man\" (2001).

Sociopolitical Commentary: E.g., \"Politics is strife of interests
masquerading as a contest of principles\" (2000).

4\. Grammar Exercises

Repeating Topics:

Voice Change: Active ↔ Passive (e.g., \"The President inaugurated the
Motorway\" → Passive).

Reported Speech: Convert direct to indirect speech (e.g., \"He said,
\'Let\'s wait\'\" → He suggested waiting).

Sentence Correction: Fix grammatical errors (e.g., \"Neither he nor I
are\" → am).

5\. Vocabulary

Repeating Question Types:

Synonyms/Antonyms: E.g., \"ARCHIPELAGO: Cluster of islands\" (2004).

Idioms: Use in sentences (e.g., \"kick the bucket,\" \"bolt from the
blue\").

Word Pairs: Differentiate homophones (e.g., auger/augur, venal/venial).

6\. Dialogue Completion

Pattern: Fill gaps in conversations using idioms/phrases (e.g., \"health
first, exam second\").

Repeats: 2000, 2001, 2002.

Key Takeaways for Preparation

Précis Practice: Focus on conciseness and title creation.

Comprehension Skills: Highlight inferential thinking and
vocabulary-in-context.

Essay Themes: Study proverbs, philosophical quotes, and societal issues.

Grammar Mastery: Drill voice changes, reported speech, and
error-spotting.

Vocabulary Building: Prioritize idioms, synonyms/antonyms, and confusing
word pairs.

# 2005-2009

Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in FPSC English Exams
(2005--2009)

Note: All papers follow a 3-hour format with 100 marks (except 2009,
split into MCQs and subjective sections).

\*\*1. Précis Writing

Pattern:

Write a précis of a \~150--200 word passage and suggest a suitable title
(20+5 marks).

Passages are philosophical, analytical, or literary (e.g., art
criticism, organizational objectives, human psychology).

Repeating Themes:

2005: Psychology of emotional disorders (neuroses/psychoses).

2006: Greek philosophy vs. modern scholasticism.

2007: Impact of literature on human personality.

2008: Organizational objectives and efficiency.

2009: Art's role in emotion vs. morality (Plato/Tolstoi).

\*\*2. Comprehension Questions

Pattern:

Answer short analytical questions (3--8 marks each) based on a literary
or critical passage.

Focus on interpretation, vocabulary, and author's intent.

Repeating Skills Tested:

Main idea extraction (e.g., \"What kind of education does the writer
favor?\" -- 2007).

Contextual meaning of phrases (e.g., \"sour-grapeism\" -- 2006).

Tone and purpose (e.g., \"Why did the writer's father study the skies?\"
-- 2005).

\*\*3. Essay/Comprehensive Note (250--300 words)

Pattern:

Philosophical, moral, or societal themes with a choice of 5 topics.

Repeating Topics Across Years:

Ignorance vs. Knowledge:

2005: \"Ignorance is bliss, knowledge worry.\"

2007: \"Where ignorance is bliss, it is folly to be wise.\"

Human Behavior & Society:

2005: \"Democracy fosters mediocrity.\"

2008: \"Life without philosophy is like a ship without a rudder.\"

Art and Creativity:

2006: \"A pen becomes a clarion.\"

2009: \"Protecting freedom of expression not lies.\"

Proverbs/Idioms:

2005: \"Each man is the architect of his own destiny.\"

2008: \"The child is father of the man.\"

\*\*4. Vocabulary

Pattern:

Synonyms/Antonyms: Match words like anathema, torpor, sequester
(2005--2009).

Pairs of Words: Differentiate homophones/near-homophones (e.g.,
council/counsel, persecute/prosecute).

Repeating Words:

Synonyms: Denouement (2005), Samizdat (2006), Frugal (2009).

Antonyms: Deleterious (2005), Capricious (2007), Turbid (2005).

\*\*5. Grammar

Pattern:

Direct/Indirect Speech: Convert sentences (e.g., \"Ali said, 'I must go
to Lahore'\" -- 2005).

Sentence Correction: Fix errors in syntax, prepositions, or comparisons
(e.g., \"Your writing is inferior than him\" → to -- 2008).

Common Errors Tested:

Incorrect comparisons (greater than twenty years ago → greater than
those twenty years ago).

Dangling modifiers (Having entered his house, the door was shut).

\*\*6. Idioms and Phrases

Pattern:

Use idioms in sentences (e.g., salt something away, wear the pants in
the family -- 2005--2009).

Repeating Idioms:

Blow one's top (2008), Turncoat (2009), Letter perfect (2005).

Year-Specific Observations

2005: Emphasis on psychology (Q1) and meteorological analogies (Q2).

2006: Critiques of modern education vs. classical philosophy (Q2).

2007: Focus on industrialization and cultural erosion (Q3).

2008: Themes of organizational efficiency and spiritual revival (Q2).

2009: Debate on art's moral impact (Q2) and Western lifestyle critiques
(Q4).

Key Takeaways for Preparation

Master Précis Writing: Practice summarizing dense philosophical texts.

Build Vocabulary: Focus on synonyms/antonyms and confusing word pairs.

Analyze Philosophical Topics: Prepare arguments for common themes like
ignorance, democracy, and art.

Grammar Drills: Prioritize direct/indirect speech and common syntax
errors.

Idioms Practice: Learn 10--15 idioms yearly and their contextual usage.

# 2010-2014

Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in FPSC English Exams
(2010--2014)

Note: All papers follow a 3-hour format with 100 marks (split into MCQs
and subjective sections).

\*\*1. Précis Writing

Pattern:

Write a précis of a \~150--200 word passage and suggest a suitable title

2012: Population control and social stratification.

\*\*2. Comprehension Questions

Comparative analysis (e.g., American vs. French Revolution -- 2014).

\*\*3. Essay/Comprehensive Note (250--300 words)

Topics revolve around proverbs, societal critiques, or moral dilemmas.

Repeating Topics Across Years:

2012: \"Self done is Well done.\"

\*\*4. Vocabulary

Pattern:

species/specie).

Repeating Words:

Synonyms: Void (2010), Chronicle (2011), Bristle (2013).

Antonyms: Vulgar (2010), Hypocrisy (2011), Inclement (2013).

\*\*5. Grammar

Pattern:

Incorrect comparisons (climate of Pakistan vs. England -- 2010).

Tense inconsistencies (He has come yesterday -- 2012).

\*\*6. Idioms and Phrases

Pattern:

Use idioms in sentences (e.g., Yeoman's service, A swan song --
2010--2014).

Repeating Idioms:

Discretion is the better part of valour (2010).

A wet blanket (2011).

Wool gathering (2012).

Year-Specific Observations

2010: Emphasis on psychological analysis (envy) and historical progress

2011: Focus on knowledge vs. understanding and human unhappiness.

2012: Themes of population control and fear of death.

2013: Analysis of Confucian ethics and cultural evolution.

2014: Comparative study of revolutions and moral education.

Key Takeaways for Preparation

society, and culture.

errors.

# 2015-2019

Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in Federal Government BS-17
English Exams (2015--2019)

1\. Précis Writing (Q.2 in All Years)

Pattern:

Write a précis of \~120--130 words and suggest a title.

Topics: Philosophical/social issues (war, education, civilization),
travel experiences, climate effects.

Recurring Themes:

2015: War as a catalyst for civilization's collapse.

2016: Travelogue to Cherokee, Oklahoma.

2017: Critique of overactivity vs. the value of \"laziness.\"

2018: Impact of climate on human productivity.

2019: Modern education theories.

2\. Comprehension (Q.3 in All Years)

Pattern:

Answer analytical questions (meanings, author's intent, implications).

Passages focus on social criticism, human behavior, or literary
excerpts.

Recurring Question Types:

Phrase Meanings (e.g., \"nobler hypothesis,\" \"malignity of war\").

Author's Justification (e.g., Why trust the scientific method? Why do
resolutions fail?).

Critical Analysis (e.g., \"Is beauty an ornament?\").

Textual Evidence (e.g., \"How does the crowd behave?\" in 2019's sci-fi
passage).

3\. Grammar & Vocabulary

a\. Sentence Correction (Q.4/Q.6 in All Years)

Pattern:

Correct 5 out of 8 sentences with errors in:

Parallel structure (e.g., \"I always have and always shall be your
friend\").

Prepositions (e.g., \"arrive at our destination\").

Redundancy (e.g., \"could hardly do no better\").

Tense consistency.

b\. Analogies (Q.5a in 2015--2018)

Pattern:

Match word pairs (e.g., LION : ROAR → SNAKE : SLITHER).

Common themes: Profession-traits (e.g., SURGEON : DEXTEROUS),
cause-effect (e.g., FEARFUL : COWER).

c\. Word Pairs (Q.5b/Q.6a in All Years)

Pattern:

Differentiate homophones or similar-sounding words (e.g., adverse vs.
averse, born vs. borne).

d\. Phrase Usage (Q.5a/Q.6b in All Years)

Pattern:

Use idioms/phrases in sentences (e.g., \"beard the lion in his den,\"
\"place in the sun\").

Common phrases: Murphy's Law, smash hit, spirit away.

4\. Translation (Q.7 in 2016, 2018, 2019)

Pattern:

Translate Urdu paragraphs into English, preserving figurative/idiomatic
expressions.

Themes: Cultural heritage, historical cities (e.g., Lahore in 2018),
moral lessons.

5\. Dialogue Conversion (2016, 2018)

Pattern:

Convert direct ↔ indirect speech (e.g., 2016's dialogue between Helen
and Mr. West).

6\. Essay Writing (2015 Only)

Pattern (2015 Q.4):

Write 250--300 words on topics like \"Education for life, not
livelihood\" or \"Human nature in adversity.\"

Yearly Shifts

2015: Emphasis on essays and literary analysis.

2016--2019: Shift toward grammar drills (sentence correction,
prepositions) and translation.

2019: Introduction of science fiction comprehension (H.G. Wells-esque
passage).

Key Takeaways for Preparation

Précis Practice: Focus on condensing philosophical/social arguments.

Comprehension Skills: Analyze tone, intent, and textual evidence.

Grammar Mastery: Prepositions, parallelism, redundancy.

Vocabulary: Differentiate word pairs and use idioms contextually.

Translation: Practice Urdu-to-English with idiomatic fidelity.

# 2019-2024

### **Underlying Patterns & Repeating Questions in English (Precis & Composition) Exams (2020--2024)**

#### **Section-Wise Patterns**

1.  **Précis Writing (Q.2)**

    - **Task**: Write a précis (summary) of a given passage and suggest
      a suitable title.

    - **Topics**:

      - **2020**: Manto's literary realism and societal criticism.

      - **2021**: Nizar Hassan's documentary filmmaking and Palestinian
        identity.

      - **2022**: Ancient Greek oracles and divine communication.

      - **2023**: Freedom in education vs. societal needs.

      - **2024**: Urban green spaces and sustainable city planning.

    - **Pattern**: Passages focus on literary figures, sociopolitical
      issues, historical practices, or philosophical debates.

<!-- -->

2.  **Comprehension (Q.3)**

    - **Task**: Answer 5 questions (4 marks each) based on a passage.

    - **Topics**:

      - **2020**: Globalization's impact on economies and societies.

      - **2021**: U.S. hyperpower politics and ethical dilemmas in
        warfare.

      - **2022**: Civil society's role in fostering social trust and
        citizenship.

      - **2023**: Voluntary simplicity movement and consumerism.

      - **2024**: Technology's ethical challenges and the digital
        divide.

    - **Pattern**: Questions demand analysis of themes, definitions,
      contextual meanings, and implications. Example recurring terms:
      *digital divide, social capital, moral equivalence*.

<!-- -->

3.  **Sentence Correction (Q.4)**

    - **Task**: Correct grammatical errors in 5 out of 8 sentences.

    - **Common Errors**:

      - Incorrect prepositions (e.g., *He wrote with ink* → *in ink*).

      - Tense mismatches (e.g., *I must walk two miles* → *had to
        walk*).

      - Redundancy (e.g., *Unless you do not try* → *Unless you try*).

      - Subject-verb agreement (e.g., *John as well as Harry bear* →
        *bears*).

    - **Pattern**: Errors often involve prepositions, articles,
      redundancy, and syntax.

<!-- -->

4.  **Punctuation & Prepositions (Q.5)**

    - **(a) Punctuation**: Add commas, apostrophes, quotation marks,
      etc.

      - Example (2020): *\"don't begin with a hackneyed phrase like I
        was delighted\...\"* → Add punctuation for clarity.

    - **(b) Prepositions**: Fill blanks with correct prepositions (e.g.,
      *annoyed **at** him*, *bound **for** Gujarat*).

    - **Pattern**: Focus on nuanced prepositions (*on, in, by, for*) and
      complex punctuation in long sentences.

<!-- -->

5.  **Word Usage (Q.6)**

    - **Task**: Use word pairs in sentences to illustrate meanings.

    - **Examples**:

      - **2020**: *Nip in the bud* vs. *Break the ice*.

      - **2021**: *Gibe* vs. *Jibe*.

      - **2023**: *Antic* vs. *Antique*.

      - **2024**: *Creak* vs. *Creek*.

    - **Pattern**: Tests homophones, idioms, and nuanced vocabulary.

<!-- -->

6.  **Translation (Q.7)**

    - **Task**: Translate Urdu/Arabic text into English, preserving
      figurative/idiomatic expressions.

    - **Pattern**: Requires understanding cultural idioms (e.g., [خوش
      آمدانہ]{dir="rtl"} → *hypocritical welcome*) and contextual
      fluency.

#### **Recurring Themes & Topics**

- **Sociopolitical Issues**: Globalization (2020), hyperpower politics
  (2021), civil society (2022), consumerism (2023).

- **Ethics & Philosophy**: Moral equivalence (2021), voluntary
  simplicity (2023), digital ethics (2024).

- **Literary/Historical Analysis**: Manto (2020), Greek oracles (2022),
  Palestinian cinema (2021).

- **Grammar Fundamentals**: Prepositions, punctuation, tense agreement,
  redundancy.

#### **Year-Specific Highlights**

- **2020**: Emphasis on globalization and literary criticism.

- **2021**: Focus on geopolitical ethics (U.S. hyperpower) and
  documentary filmmaking.

- **2022**: Exploration of ancient religious practices and civil society
  dynamics.

- **2023**: Debates on education philosophy and anti-consumerism.

- **2024**: Urban sustainability and technology's ethical challenges.
