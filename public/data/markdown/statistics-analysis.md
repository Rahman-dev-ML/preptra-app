# 2001-2005

### Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in Statistics Exams (2001--2005)

#### 1. Probability Theory

- **Recurring Topics**:

  - Classical, axiomatic, and relative frequency definitions of
    probability (2001, 2004).

  - Law of total probability, Bayes' theorem, conditional probability
    (2001, 2003, 2004).

  - Mutually exclusive vs. independent events (2001, 2003, 2004).

- **Examples**:

  - 2001 Q1a: Definitions of probability with examples.

  - 2004 Q1c: Proof that mutually exclusive events cannot be
    independent.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 2. Probability Distributions

- **Key Distributions**:

  - **Normal**: Properties (mean = median = mode), applications (2001,
    2002, 2003, 2004, 2005).

  - **Binomial vs. Poisson**: Comparisons, variance derivations (2001,
    2002, 2004, 2005).

  - **Negative Binomial/Hypergeometric**: Definitions, mean/variance
    (2001, 2003, 2004).

- **Examples**:

  - 2003 Q3a: Hypergeometric probabilities summing to unity.

  - 2005 Q8(3): Binomial distribution properties (mean vs. variance).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 3. Hypothesis Testing

- **Common Tests**:

  - **t-test/z-test**: Comparing means (2003, 2004, 2005).

  - **Chi-square**: Independence, goodness-of-fit (2001, 2002, 2005).

  - **ANOVA**: Testing equality of means (2002, 2004, 2005).

- **Examples**:

  - 2001 Q7: Chi-square test for marital status and absenteeism.

  - 2005 Q3b: t-test for pedestrian fatalities across time periods.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 4. Regression & Correlation

- **Key Topics**:

  - Regression coefficients, interpretation of slope/intercept (2001,
    2003, 2004, 2005).

  - Coefficient of determination ((r\^2)) and partial correlation (2002,
    2004, 2005).

- **Examples**:

  - 2001 Q5a: Regression line definitions and relationship with
    correlation.

  - 2004 Q4a: Calculating correlation coefficient and regression line.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 5. Sampling Techniques

- **Frequent Methods**:

  - Stratified, cluster, systematic sampling (2001, 2002, 2003, 2004,
    2005).

  - Comparisons: Stratum vs. cluster, precision vs. reliability (2004,
    2005).

- **Examples**:

  - 2002 Q5: Advantages of cluster sampling.

  - 2005 Q5a: Procedure for stratified random sampling.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 6. Statistical Inference

- **Key Concepts**:

  - Maximum likelihood estimation (2001, 2003, 2004).

  - Properties of estimators (unbiasedness, consistency) (2004, 2005).

- **Examples**:

  - 2001 Q4b: MLE for exponential distribution.

  - 2004 Q3b: Desirable properties of point estimators.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 7. Compulsory MCQ/True-False Sections

- **Repeated Concepts**:

  - **Standard error** (2004 Q8(1), **Poisson mean = variance** (2004
    Q8(3)).

  - **Skewness** (2004 Q8(6): Mean \> median \> mode in positive skew.

  - **ANOVA assumptions** (2003 Q8e, 2004 Q6b).

- **Examples**:

  - 2003 Q8: \"Random variable is always a real-valued function\"
    (True).

  - 2005 Q8(7): \"Coefficient of determination = (r\^2)\" (True).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

#### 8. Short Notes

- **Frequent Topics**:

  - ANOVA, Bayes' theorem, multiple/partial correlation (2002, 2003,
    2004, 2005).

  - Disadvantages of sampling, normal distribution importance (2002,
    2004).

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

### Summary of Patterns

1.  **Core Focus**: Probability, distributions (normal, binomial,
    Poisson), hypothesis testing, regression, and sampling methods
    dominate all exams.

2.  **Application Emphasis**: Real-world scenarios (public health,
    socio-economics, manufacturing) are common in questions.

3.  **Yearly Structure**: Compulsory MCQs test foundational concepts,
    while long questions blend theory and application.

4.  **Repeat Topics**:

    a.  Normal distribution properties (all years).

    b.  Regression line interpretation (2001--2005).

    c.  Stratified vs. cluster sampling (2001--2005).

### Key Takeaways for Preparation

1.  **Master Regression Analysis**: Focus on interpreting ( r\^2 ),
    computing coefficients, and hypothesis testing for slopes.

2.  **Practice Probability Scenarios**: Urn problems, conditional
    probability, and hypergeometric/Poisson distributions.

3.  **Understand Sampling Distributions**: Variance formulas and proofs
    (especially for finite populations).

4.  **ANOVA and Experimental Design**: RCBD, Latin Square, LSD test
    applications.

5.  **Index Numbers**: Memorize Laspeyres, Paasche, and Fisher's
    formulas.

6.  **Hypothesis Testing**: T-tests, Z-tests, and confidence intervals
    for proportions/means.
