# 2001-2005

### **Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in English Literature Exams (2001-2005)**

**Focus Areas:**

1.  **Romantic Poets** (Blake, Wordsworth, Shelley, Keats, Byron):

2.  Blake's lyrical poetry and dualism in *Songs of Innocence and
    Experience* (2001, 2002, 2004, 2005).

3.  Wordsworth's philosophy of nature and humanism (2001--2005).

4.  Shelley's revolutionary ideals (*Prometheus Unbound*) and visionary
    intensity (2001, 2003, 2005).

5.  Keats' Hellenism, odes, and synaesthesia (2001--2005).

6.  **Victorian Literature** (Dickens, Hardy, Eliot, Tennyson, Ruskin):

7.  Dickens' social criticism (*Hard Times*, *Bleak House*) vs. humor
    (2001--2005).

8.  Hardy's fatalism, tragic characters, and \"manipulated endings\"
    (2001--2005).

9.  George Eliot's psychological realism (*The Mill on the Floss*)
    (2002, 2004, 2005).

10. Tennyson's craftsmanship (*In Memoriam*, *Idylls of the King*)
    (2001--2005).

11. Ruskin's social reform and art criticism (2001--2005).

12. **Modernist and 20th-Century Works** (Yeats, Eliot, Hemingway,
    Beckett):

13. Yeats' symbolism (*Sailing to Byzantium*, *The Second Coming*)
    (2002--2005).

14. Eliot's *The Waste Land* as universal vs. fragmented (2002--2005).

15. Hemingway's *The Old Man and the Sea* as allegory (2002--2005).

16. Beckett's absurdism in *Waiting for Godot* (2002, 2003).

17. **Drama** (Shakespeare, Shaw, Wilde):

18. Hamlet's delay and tragic flaws (2001, 2003, 2004, 2005).

19. Shaw's *Pygmalion* as social satire (2002, 2004, 2005).

20. Wilde's aestheticism (*The Importance of Being Earnest*) (2001,
    2003).

### **Repeating Questions (Themes & Specifics)**

#### **Romanticism**

1.  **Wordsworth's Nature and Humanity** (2001--2005):

2.  \"How does Wordsworth restore humanity in *The Prelude*?\" (2002,
    2005).

3.  Significance of Lucy poems (2001, 2003).

4.  **Keats' Odes and Hellenism** (2001--2005):

5.  \"Ode on a Grecian Urn\" and \"Beauty is truth, truth beauty\"
    (2001, 2004).

6.  Synaesthesia in Keats' poetry (2003).

7.  **Shelley's Revolutionary Vision** (2001--2005):

8.  *Prometheus Unbound* as an allegory of emancipation (2002, 2005).

#### **Victorian Era**

1.  **Dickens' Dual Role** (2001--2005):

2.  \"Are Dickens' humor and social reform complementary?\" (2003,
    2004).

3.  Utilitarianism in *Hard Times* (2001, 2004).

4.  **Hardy's Tragic Universe** (2001--2005):

5.  Fate vs. social forces in novels (2001, 2003, 2004).

6.  \"Gloom intensified in later novels\" (2004).

7.  **Tennyson's Craftsmanship** (2001--2005):

8.  *In Memoriam* as elegy (2001, 2003).

#### **Modernism**

1.  **Yeats' Symbolism** (2002--2005):

2.  Byzantium as a symbol (2002, 2004).

3.  \"Terrible beauty\" in *Easter 1916* (2002).

4.  **Eliot's *The Waste Land*** (2002--2005):

5.  \"Is it a public or private poem?\" (2002, 2003).

#### **Compulsory MCQ Repetitions**

1.  **Authorship Identification**:

2.  \"Which Dickens novel critiques utilitarianism?\" (*Hard Times*)
    (2001, 2004).

3.  \"Who wrote *The Rubaiyat of Omar Khayyam*?\" (Edward Fitzgerald)
    (2001).

4.  **Quotes & Poems**:

5.  \"Beauty is truth, truth beauty\" (Keats' *Ode on a Grecian Urn*)
    (2001, 2004).

6.  \"Spontaneous overflow of powerful feelings\" (Wordsworth) (2002,
    2003, 2004).

7.  **Literary Terms**:

8.  Dramatic monologue (Browning) vs. soliloquy (Shakespeare) (2001,
    2003).

9.  Oxymoron vs. paradox (2003, 2005).

### **Key Observations**

1.  **Romantic vs. Victorian Contrast**: Repeatedly asked (2003, 2005).

2.  **Hamlet's Delay**: Appears in 2001, 2003, 2004, 2005.

3.  **Social Criticism**: Ruskin, Dickens, and Shaw are recurring
    (2001--2005).

4.  **MCQ Overlaps**: Questions on *The Waste Land*, *Prelude*, and
    Hardy's fatalism repeat verbatim.

**Years with Notable Repetitions**:

1.  **2002 & 2004**: Focus on Wordsworth's *Prelude* and Keats' odes.

2.  **2003 & 2005**: Repeated questions on Eliot's universality and
    Frost's poetry.

This pattern suggests a strong emphasis on **core authors**, **key
works**, and **critical perspectives** across eras, with compulsory
questions testing rote memorization of facts.

# 2009-2013

### **Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in English Literature Exams (2009--2013)**

#### **1. Romantic Poets (Wordsworth, Shelley, Keats, Byron)**

1.  **Pattern**: Frequent focus on their philosophies, major works, and
    contributions to Romanticism.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  **Wordsworth's Nature Philosophy** (2009 Q4, 2011 Q4, 2013 Q2).

4.  **Keats's Sensuality vs. Intellect** (2009 Q5, 2010 Q5, 2011 Q5,
    2012 Q3).

5.  **Shelley's Lyrical vs. Intellectual Genius** (2009 Q3, 2010 Q3,
    2011 Q3).

6.  **Byron's Emotional Exploitation** (2010 Q4).

#### **2. Victorian Literature (Dickens, George Eliot, Hardy, Browning)**

1.  **Pattern**: Emphasis on social criticism, character analysis, and
    Victorian societal themes.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  **Dickens as a Social Novelist** (2009 Q7, 2010 Q8, 2011 Q6, 2013
    Q7).

4.  **Hardy's Meliorism vs. Pessimism** (2009 Q6, 2011 Q7, 2012 Q7, 2013
    Q8).

5.  **Browning's Dramatic Monologues** (2009 Q8, 2010 Q6, 2011 Q8).

#### **3. Shakespearean Tragedies (Hamlet)**

1.  **Pattern**: Analysis of Hamlet's existential crisis, madness, and
    foils.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  **Hamlet's Madness/Character** (2009 Q2, 2010 Q2, 2012 Q2, 2013 Q2).

4.  **Frailty, Thy Name is Woman** (2010 Q2, 2011 Q2).

#### **4. Modernist Works (Eliot, Yeats, Beckett)**

1.  **Pattern**: Focus on existential themes, symbolism, and literary
    techniques.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  **Yeats's \"The Second Coming\"** (2009 Q8, 2010 Q8, 2012 Q7, 2013
    Q5).

4.  **Eliot's \"The Waste Land\"** (2012 Q4, 2013 Q6).

5.  **Beckett's \"Waiting for Godot\"** (2009 Q3, 2010 Q3, 2013 Q8).

#### **5. Jane Austen's Novels**

1.  **Pattern**: Character analysis and social satire in *Pride and
    Prejudice*.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  **Significance of Title \"Pride and Prejudice\"** (2010 Q7, 2012
    Q8).

4.  **Austen's Satire vs. Snobbery** (2013 Q4).

#### **6. Swift's *Gulliver's Travels***

1.  **Pattern**: Satire, political critique, and symbolism.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  **Swift's Satire in the Fourth Voyage** (2009 Q5, 2010 Q5, 2012 Q5,
    2013 Q3).

4.  **Gulliver as a Symbol of Alienation** (2011 Q5, 2013 Q3).

#### **7. Literary Devices and Terms**

1.  **Pattern**: Definitions and applications (e.g., negative
    capability, pathetic fallacy).

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  **Negative Capability** (2009 Q7, 2011 MCQs).

4.  **Synaesthesia** (2009 Q18, 2010 Q18).

#### **8. Charles Lamb's Essays**

1.  **Pattern**: Humanism and reflection of London life.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  **Lamb's Essays as \"Lyric Poems in Prose\"** (2009 Q7, 2012 Q2,
    2013 Q4).

#### **9. George Bernard Shaw's Plays**

1.  **Pattern**: Critique of societal norms and heroism.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  **\"Pygmalion\" and Modern Relevance** (2010 Q4, 2012 Q3, 2013 Q3).

4.  **Shaw's Anti-Heroism in *Arms and the Man*** (2011 Q3).

#### **10. Hemingway's Themes**

1.  **Pattern**: Heroism and existential struggle.

2.  **Repeating Questions**:

3.  **\"A Man Can Be Destroyed But Not Defeated\"** (2012 Q6, 2013 Q7).

### **Summary of Key Recurrences:**

1.  **Romanticism vs. Victorian Realism**: Repeated across all years.

2.  **Hamlet's Existential Crisis**: A staple in Paper II.

3.  **Yeats's \"The Second Coming\"**: Asked in 4/5 years.

4.  **Dickens' Social Criticism**: Consistent focus on *Oliver Twist*,
    *Great Expectations*.

5.  **Austen's *Pride and Prejudice***: Analyzed for characterization
    and satire in all years.

**Years with Highest Overlap**: 2009, 2010, and 2013 (shared themes in
Romanticism, Victorian prose, and Modernist works).

# 2019-2024

### **Underlying Patterns and Repeating Questions in English Literature Exams (2019--2024)**

Below are the recurring themes, authors, texts, and question types
observed across the past papers:

### **1. Frequently Tested Authors and Texts**

#### **Bertrand Russell's *The Conquest of Happiness***

1.  **Years:** 2019, 2023, 2024

2.  **Patterns:**Connection between envy/unhappiness (2024 MCQ).

3.  Modern applicability of happiness (2019 Q2, 2023 Q2).

2.  **Patterns:**Coexistence of pleasure and pain in *Ode to a

### **Key Takeaways for Preparation**

    texts/themes.

4.  **Link Classics to Modernity**: Be ready to connect texts like
    *1984* or *Hamlet* to contemporary issues.

By prioritizing these patterns, candidates can streamline their revision
and tackle high-frequency topics effectively.
