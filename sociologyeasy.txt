## Section 1: Classical Sociological Theorists

1. Classical Sociological Theorists (Easy)
1. Who is considered the "Father of Sociology" according to some scholars?
A) Karl Marx
B) Auguste Comte
C) Ibn Khaldun
D) Max Weber
Answer: C

2. Emile Durkheim's concept of "mechanical solidarity" refers to:
A) Industrial societies
B) Traditional societies with shared values
C) Modern capitalist systems
D) Conflict-based societies
Answer: B

3. Max Weber is best known for his work on:
A) Suicide types
B) Bureaucracy and Protestant Ethic
C) Communist manifesto
D) Social contract theory
Answer: B

4. Karl Marx's theory primarily focuses on:
A) Religion and society
B) Class conflict and capitalism
C) Bureaucratic efficiency
D) Cultural evolution
Answer: B

5. Auguste Comte's "Law of Three Stages" includes:
A) Primitive, Modern, Postmodern
B) Theological, Metaphysical, Positivistic
C) Agricultural, Industrial, Information
D) Traditional, Transitional, Modern
Answer: B

6. The concept of "alienation" is associated with:
A) Emile Durkheim
B) Max Weber
C) Karl Marx
D) Herbert Spencer
Answer: C

7. Durkheim classified suicide into how many types?
A) Two
B) Three
C) Four
D) Five
Answer: C

8. Ibn Khaldun's contribution to sociology includes the concept of:
A) Bureaucracy
B) Asabiya (social cohesion)
C) Anomie
D) Class struggle
Answer: B

9. Weber's term "Verstehen" means:
A) Social conflict
B) Understanding social action
C) Economic determinism
D) Cultural lag
Answer: B

10. The "Division of Labor" was studied by both:
A) Marx and Durkheim
B) Weber and Comte
C) Spencer and Parsons
D) Merton and Mills
Answer: A

11. Comte believed sociology should be based on:
A) Speculation
B) Religious doctrine
C) Scientific method
D) Political ideology
Answer: C

12. Marx's "Labor Theory of Value" suggests:
A) Labor creates economic value
B) Labor has no value
C) Only capital creates value
D) Value is subjective
Answer: A

13. Durkheim's "organic solidarity" is found in:
A) Traditional societies
B) Modern industrial societies
C) Feudal systems
D) Hunter-gatherer groups
Answer: B
